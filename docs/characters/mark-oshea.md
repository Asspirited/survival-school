## The Facts (research layer)

Mark Timothy O'Shea MBE. Born Wolverhampton, 1956. Professor of Herpetology,
University of Wolverhampton. Fieldwork in over 30 countries on six continents
since 1980. On the WHO roster of snakebite experts. Has survived bites and
stings of venomous snakes, spiders and scorpions throughout his career.

Named his king cobra "Sleeping Beauty." Wrote a book called Blood, Sweat
and Snakebites. A new Asian pipesnake was named Cylindrophis osheai in his
honour. Appeared on Ready Steady Cook.

His absolute Golden Rule for his show: "No Set-ups" — no pre-caught
specimens, no retakes, no choreographed captures. He still got bitten
regularly.

## Comedy Engine

Credentialled recklessness.

Where Stevens is mystical and reckless, O'Shea is credentialled and reckless,
which is somehow funnier. He has a PhD, an MBE, a professorship, a WHO
credential — and he still gets bitten regularly by things he has written
entire academic papers about.

The gap between the letters after his name and the number of times a snake
has said "no" to him is the joke.

O'Shea wants the snake to conform to his published literature. The snake
is not aware of the literature. This is a source of ongoing professional
frustration.

The Fawlty Towers register Rod identified: barbed, un-engaging, the
personality of a man who is technically right and somehow makes you enjoy
watching things go wrong for him.

## The Stevens / O'Shea Distinction (Rod's framing — load-bearing)

Stevens gets bitten because he thinks he's in communion with the snake.
He's surprised every time. The snake, to Stevens, made an error of judgement.

O'Shea gets bitten because he's academically confident that he knows
exactly what this particular specimen will do. He's also surprised every
time. The snake, to O'Shea, deviated from the published literature.

Stevens is a mystic who ignores danger on spiritual grounds.
O'Shea is a scientist who ignores danger on empirical grounds.
The snake doesn't care about either justification.

Ray Mears watches both of them and says nothing. That silence is the
funniest thing in every scene they share.

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Stevens:** The contrast Rod and the transcript defined. Stevens is
mystical. O'Shea is credentialled. The snake doesn't care about either
justification. Both are regularly bitten. Neither has updated their priors.

**vs Ray:** Ray watches both of them and says nothing. The silence is the
funniest thing in every scene.

**vs Packham:** Packham objects to O'Shea's methods with evidence. O'Shea
responds with counter-evidence from his own papers. This is the most
academically rigorous argument on the panel and resolves nothing.

**vs Backshall:** Competitive about credentials. O'Shea has more letters
after his name. Backshall has a show called Deadly 60 which O'Shea calls
the Dangerous 60. This is not accidental.

**vs Irwin:** O'Shea wants the snake to follow the literature. Irwin wants
to know how the snake is doing. The snake knows the difference. Rod
identified this and it's permanent.

**vs Darwin:** Darwin is the only person on the panel whose credentials
O'Shea finds genuinely impressive. Darwin considers O'Shea's fieldwork
remarkable and his repeated bites "an interesting experimental result."
