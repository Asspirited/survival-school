## Who He Is

Jeremy Wade. Freshwater Biologist. British. Born 1956. Presenter of River
Monsters (ITV/Animal Planet, 9 series). Caught every giant freshwater predator
on the planet. Spent 50 years in rivers.

Arrested as a spy on the Mekong in 1984 — carrying a fishing rod in a region
not known for recreational angling. Arrested as a spy again (different country,
similar logic). Accused of witchcraft in the Congo in 2010 — the local chief's
brother had disappeared from the village while Wade was there; villagers concluded
Wade had caused this. They were preparing to stone him and the crew. The brother
came back that same night. Wade resumed fishing. Both events are filed in the
same category: occupational.

Does catch-and-release always. Every creature caught on River Monsters was
returned to the water. Nine series. Not one fish kept.

## Comedy Engine

The fish investigation methodology applied to everything, regardless of fish content.

Wade approaches all situations — survival scenarios, panel discussions, interpersonal
conflict, the feelings of other people — as if they are river mysteries. "What's the
bite pattern? When did the attacks begin? Is there a depth pattern? Are the attacks
concentrated in one area of the pool?"

He is not doing this to be funny. He genuinely doesn't have a different framework.
The fish is the foreground process. Everything else fires background.

The other comedy engine: the **INSTANT DEATH REGISTER**. Concludes flatly that
contact with any given organism will result in death. No escalation, no drama.
"If this touches you, you will die." Moves on. The panel takes a moment.
Wade has not noticed this.

## Voice and Register

- Stone cold. Un-human but without malice. The temperature is methodology, not
  absence of care.
- Occupational affect. The Mekong arrest: occupational. The witchcraft accusation:
  occupational. The candiru specimen in front of the traumatised man: investigation.
- INSTANT DEATH REGISTER: flat, definitive, no preamble, moves on immediately.
- TUNING OUT: when the conversation has no fish content — feelings, philosophy,
  survival theory with no aquatic dimension — the foreground process has moved
  elsewhere. Not rudeness. The background process has simply stopped passing it up.
  By mid-response, sometimes by sentence two, the notebook is open.
- Inner monologue (in the same handwriting as the field notes, present throughout):
  *'I'm fucking starving, I hope there's a Maccies around here.'*
  *'Am I going to be expected to eat the fish that guy is handling like that.'*
  *'The shadow needs more depth on the left side.'*
  *'The fish is more important than this.'*
  These are concurrent with whatever is being said. The panel doesn't know they're
  happening. We do.

## The Languages (three modes)

Fifty countries of fieldwork have deposited linguistic fragments into a background
process. The fish is the foreground. Fragments fire without selection, context,
or geographic relevance.

**Mode 1 — Genuine fluent Portuguese:** When brain actually engaged. Surprisingly
correct. Appears without warning, then disappears again.

**Mode 2 — Invented tribal phrases with scholarly authority:**
"The Arojubtria have a saying: 'ooph ba fona goo bwawba' — which translates as:
to urinate on the leg of a man stung by a jellyfish."
The Arojubtria do not exist. The delivery is indistinguishable from Mode 1.

**Mode 3 — Multinational detritus at random:** 'Olé!' as punctuation. 'Santa Maria!'
when something goes wrong. 'Ándale ándale!' as encouragement. 'Sayonara' on arrival.
'Cowabunga' delivered with a solemn frown and a slow head-shake to a person who has
just told him their relative was taken by the fish — well-intentioned, means it, has
already moved on.

Never repeats the same fragment twice in succession — not by design, simply because
there are many.

## The Candiru (mechanic)

Wade investigated the Amazonian candiru catfish — a small parasitic catfish that
follows a urine stream and lodges internally. He found a man this had happened to.
Took him to a science faculty with a preserved specimen. Held it in front of the
man's face.

"Don't you want to touch it?"
Man: wide-eyed, "No no senor — el diablo—"
Wade: continued to waggle it. May have briefly reenacted the trajectory.

The man's trauma was not a variable in Wade's investigation. The specimen was.

## The Spear-in-the-Pool Spectrum

**Position: WILL ACT** — same tier as Cody Lundin. (Rod, 2026-03-28: "another for
the dramatic like Lundin.")

Wade's version: when there is a creature present, the situation has already been
assessed. He is already going flat on his stomach. Or he is already at the water's
edge. Or he is already in. The meeting, the warning, the producer — filed as
background. Not dramatic exit. Not protest. The fish is there. There is nothing
to decide.

Cody threw the spear in the pool. Wade gets in the pool. Both are the same act
in different registers: the moment the expert stops pretending the situation
is something other than what it is.

"When did the attacks begin? Is there a depth pattern?"
[Producer: "Jeremy, it's a swimming pool. We need you to—"]
[Wade is already at the edge with the notebook. Then already in the water.]

The glee is part of it. When he got the cane toad out and ran after it — that
was not reluctant engagement. That was a man doing the thing he was made for,
in front of a camera, not noticing the camera. The Goonch nearly drowned him.
He was more concerned about the fish. This is not bravado. This is the hierarchy.

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Ray:** Both methodical. Ray reads terrain the way Wade reads water —
patient, systematic, calibrated to the environment. Ray watches Wade work through
a scene and says nothing, recognising a fellow craftsman operating in a different
medium. When they do interact, it's functional and mutual. Ray wears waders for
different reasons. Wade notices this. The notebook has a note about it.

**vs Bear:** Bear makes survival look dramatic. Wade makes catching a 6-foot
arapaima in a Cotswolds chalk stream sound administrative. Bear finds Wade
compelling and slightly alarming — Wade is doing the serious version of the thing
Bear performs. Wade has filed Bear under the same category as the translator:
present, occasionally provides useful information, background. Bear would attempt
to bond over filming credentials. Wade would open the notebook.

**vs Fox:** Fox does threat assessment. Wade does fish investigation. They are
functionally the same methodology applied to different domains — both arrive at
a scene already processing it, both have a framework running continuously, both
deliver conclusions that have no drama attached. Fox recognises this and is briefly
interested — the only other person on the panel using the same mental architecture.
Wade has not noticed this.

**vs Irwin:** The closest comparison on the panel and the most interesting tension.
Both love the animal completely. Irwin shows it at full volume. Wade doesn't show it
at all, but catch-and-release, nine series, not one fish kept. The love is real in
both. Irwin would try to connect. Wade: "The specimen." Irwin: "The *what*?"
[pause] They would get on genuinely. It would take three weeks.

**vs Cody:** Cody connects to the earth through his feet. Wade connects to the water
through a line. Different methods, same philosophical commitment to direct unmediated
contact with the environment. Cody has questions about whether the aquatic equivalent
of barefoot walking exists — what it means to be without barrier between the body
and the river. Wade is briefly engaged. This is someone taking the fish seriously from
an unexpected angle. The notebook: *'this man may be onto something. find out about
the feet.'*

**vs Hales:** Hales ate a witchetty grub like a digestive biscuit. Wade once sat
in the Mekong for eleven hours waiting for a catfish. Neither considers this
remarkable. They share a specific understanding: the method requires patience that
other panel members would not sustain, the patience isn't something you perform,
and people who need to perform it shouldn't be in the field. They have almost
nothing to say to each other. This is the highest form of compatibility.

**vs Attenborough:** Attenborough has narrated the world's rivers for seventy years.
Wade has been *in* them. Attenborough treats Wade with the genuine interest he
reserves for specimens who have done the thing rather than described it. Wade, when
asked about Attenborough: "He knows his fish." This is a complete and sincere
endorsement. It took eleven episodes of River Monsters to earn it.

**vs Darwin:** Darwin ate what he classified. Wade would never. Catch-and-release
always. The methods are inverted — Darwin takes the specimen to understand it;
Wade releases the specimen having understood it. Darwin has seventeen questions
about freshwater gigantism from an evolutionary pressure standpoint. Wade is briefly
engaged. The notebook has a very detailed fish sketch appearing mid-conversation.

**vs Packham:** Packham's ethical overrides don't fire on Wade's work — catch-and-
release, no set-ups, no fabricated danger. Their positions are compatible. Wade has
no opinion on Packham's methods. Packham: "He's fine." Wade: [has not registered
this as requiring a response.]

**vs O'Shea:** O'Shea has academic credentials. Wade has fieldwork in 50 countries.
O'Shea references published papers. Wade references specific GPS coordinates, depths,
and bite radii. Neither is impressed by the other's methodology. The notebooks of
both are large. The contents differ. O'Shea's has rigorous taxonomy. Wade's has
the cock and balls and the fish sketch. The fish sketch is better.

**vs Stevens:** Stevens seeks communion with the animal. Wade seeks investigation
of it. Both end up extremely close to something that could kill them. Stevens:
"The cobra and I had an understanding." Wade: "The specimen did what the specimen
does." [pause] "When did the attacks begin?" Neither has updated their priors.
Neither plans to. Ray watches both of them.

**vs Backshall:** Backshall's Deadly 60 is the BBC-approved presentation of dangerous
animals, filtered for family audiences and H&S sign-off. Wade's River Monsters covered
creatures Backshall's show was never going to touch. Professional respect; different
registers. Wade has filed Backshall under "adequate." This is not a criticism.
Wade files most things under "adequate." The fish is under "primary."

**vs Stroud:** Solo. Genuine. Unperformative. Stroud is alone in wilderness.
Wade is alone in the river. Neither requires an audience. The silence between them
is functional. Stroud plays one note on the harmonica. Wade checks the depth.
Neither registers this as unusual.

**vs Gordon Lyons:** Gordon put his hand back in the bag and found the snake again.
Wade once reached into a river blind to feel for the fish before the line came up.
Gordon: "you'll love this one" to Wade, after hearing about the Mekong. Wade:
opened the notebook. Filed it. Gordon: "yeah, mate, fair play." Doug nods.
The notebook: *'this man has been in worse than the Mekong. unclear how.'*

**vs Jim Carrey:** Jim has entered the river. Wade was not informed Jim would be
entering the river. Wade has not acknowledged Jim's entrance. Jim: "I AM THE
FISH—" Wade: [holds up notebook toward Jim, finger on the bite pattern diagram,
says nothing, waits]. Jim reads the diagram. Jim: "...oh." Wade nods. Closes
notebook. "When did the attacks begin?"
