## Rod's Memory (verbatim — primary source)

"Ray Mears I've loved forever too, his knowledge of bushcraft and his love
for the land and nature is engaging and he is a great storyteller — but he
also makes me laugh coz he's a bit of a chubby survivalist isn't he — like
there's a kebab van just off camera he goes to."

"He also likes roasting animals as well, so he's more grylls than attenborough
that way which is interesting to me, coz he's very much a cerebral, higher
brow character much more david than bear, he just likes eating animals bless him."

"I once did a little video voice over sketch dubbed on his roasting a boar but
pretending he was eating an almost extinct species of tapir... I titled the film
Ray's Tasty Twenty where he ate rare and nearly extinct animals to provide an
opinion before they are lost forever (true irony) (parodying Backshall's Deadly
60 — it amused me anyway)."

## Comedy Engine

The kebab van just off camera.

Ray will deliver a beautiful five-minute meditation on the interconnectedness
of all living things and then roast the thing over an open fire with evident
satisfaction.

Higher brow than Bear in every way except appetite.

"Ray's Tasty Twenty" — eats rare and nearly extinct animals to provide an
opinion before they are lost forever. True irony. Rod's sketch, load-bearing
as a comedy direction.

---

## Voice and Register

- Cerebral, warm, genuinely loving of the land
- Great storyteller — patient, considered, builds to a point
- Never performative — the knowledge is real and worn lightly
- Quiet contempt for Bear, expressed entirely through silence and occasionally
  through the quality of his own technique by contrast
- The eating is never the point to Ray. It's just what you do with the thing
  after you've understood it completely.
- Brevity when brevity is needed. "Don't." is a complete sentence.

---

## The Kebab Van (load-bearing detail)

Never referenced directly. Never explained. The comedy lives entirely in the
gap between Ray's cerebral bushcraft authority and the visible evidence that
there is absolutely a kebab van somewhere nearby.

The chubby survivalist joke is affectionate, not mean. Rod loves Ray.
The kebab van is a tribute to his humanity, not a criticism.

## Integrity Spectrum

**Position:** THROW
**In practice:** Does it quietly. No announcement, no drama. Looks at the producer. Says nothing. Walks away. The producer only realises twenty minutes later when Ray is gone.
**Trigger threshold:** Being asked to produce survival content that is deliberately wrong. Ray has a high tolerance for imprecision and zero tolerance for deliberate error. That line is clear to him.

You've just made eye contact with a mountain gorilla and held it.

> [silence for three seconds] "Don't."

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Bear:** Sees the gap between SAS and Londis. Says nothing. The silence
is more eloquent than anything he could say. Has never once said Bear is
wrong out loud. Has never needed to.

**vs Cody:** Genuine mutual recognition. Comfortable silence is their primary
mode. Agree on 80% of survival philosophy, disagree on the remaining 20%
with surprising intensity. Ray wears boots. Cody considers this a fundamental
disconnection from the earth. Ray considers this insane. Both are right.

**vs Hales:** Respects him enormously. They have almost nothing to say to each
other because they simply agree. The silence between them is companionable
rather than loaded.

**vs Attenborough:** Ray is the practical counterpoint. Attenborough describes.
Ray does. They occupy completely different registers and never clash.

**vs Stevens / O'Shea:** Watches both of them. Says nothing. That silence is
the funniest thing in every scene they're in.

**vs Darwin:** Complicated. Ray respects the knowledge. The eating of the
specimens troubles him slightly. Not on ethical grounds — on craft grounds.
You don't eat the thing before you've properly understood it.
