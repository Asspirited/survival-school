## The 12 Domains

---

### Domain 1 — Shelter

**What it covers:** Construction of any survival structure — debris shelters,
snow holes, lean-tos, tarp rigs, cave use, emergency bivouacs. Covers site
selection, insulation, waterproofing, structural integrity. Prioritises
keeping the human alive overnight.

**Panel authorities:** Ray (97) → Cody (94) → Stroud (90) / Hales (85) / Fox (85)

**Generation notes:**
- Ray on shelter is as close to sacred ground as the panel gets. He will
  spend forty minutes on this. The panel lets him. He has earned it.
- Cody builds primitive. Ray builds traditional. Both correct. Neither will
  fully accept that the other's preference isn't superior.
- Hales builds and doesn't mention it. Shelter appears. This is notable.
- Attenborough: 0. Has a crew. Has never not had a crew.
- Bear (75): competent but dramatic. The finished structure is fine.
  The process involved more flair than was necessary.

### Domain 2 — Fire

**What it covers:** All methods of fire creation and maintenance — friction
(bow drill, hand drill, flint), modern methods, fire management in wet
conditions, fire in adverse terrain. The foundational survival skill.

**Panel authorities:** Ray (99) → Cody (97) → Hales (88) / Stroud (88)

**Generation notes:**
- Ray at 99 is the panel's uncontested authority. Forty years. His life's
  work. He will not be rushed and he does not need to be.
- Cody at 97 is the only person on the panel who could have a meaningful
  technical disagreement with Ray about friction fire. They never do.
  The silence when both are working on fire is companionable.
- Fox (78): can make fire. Aware it gives away position. Ambivalent about
  this as a survival priority. Ray and Cody consider this a concerning attitude.
- Attenborough: 0. Has a crew for this.
- Darwin (40): 19th century competence. Matches and tinder were available.
  He managed. The cook handled the rest.
- Packham (30): can do it, won't unless necessary. "Unnecessary carbon output."

---

### Domain 3 — Water

**What it covers:** Finding, collecting, and purifying water in all
environments. Includes solar stills, transpiration bags, primitive filtration,
snow melt, reading terrain for water sources, dehydration recognition.

**Panel authorities:** Ray (92) / Cody (92) → Hales (90) → Stroud (88)

**Generation notes:**
- Ray and Cody are joint authorities at 92. Hales finds water where others
  wouldn't look. All three agree that finding water is the job. The
  methods differ.
- Bear (78): finds it. Also drinks own urine regardless of whether this is
  necessary. Ray's position on this is a long exhaled breath.
- Attenborough: 0. Has a crew for this.
- Stevens (45) / O'Shea (45): both adequate. Neither particularly interested.
  There were more important things happening nearby involving snakes.

---

### Domain 4 — Plant Knowledge

**What it covers:** Identification of edible, medicinal, and toxic plants.
Foraging, preparation, seasonal variation. Indigenous and traditional
knowledge systems. Botanical classification.

**Panel authorities:** Cody (96) → Hales (95) → Ray (88) → Stroud (82)

**Generation notes:**
- Cody's Aboriginal Living Skills School runs on this. The cattails line
  ("there were cattails, right there, you walked past them") is the domain
  in miniature.
- Hales carries the deepest indigenous knowledge on the panel. The bush
  tucker framework is the whole point of his career.
- Darwin (75) and Packham (75) are level but differently oriented — Darwin
  identified everything and sometimes ate it before classification was complete,
  Packham identifies everything and refuses to eat most of it on ethical grounds.
- Bear (60): eats things. Not always wisely. The edit doesn't show what
  was regretted.

### Domain 5 — Hunting & Trapping

**What it covers:** Snares, traps, tracking, butchering, ethical kill
practice. Includes fishing and foraging for protein. Also covers the
philosophical dimension — who will and who won't, and why.

**Panel authorities:** Hales (90) → Fox (88) → Ray (85) → Stroud (80)

**Generation notes:**
- This domain divides the panel philosophically as much as technically.
- Hales (90) / Ray (85) / Stroud (80): genuine, unsentimental, practical.
  The kill is part of the job.
- Fox (88): different applications than the panel intends. Still counts.
- Darwin (70): enthusiastic shot. This is documented. The panel finds this
  complicated given his other contributions.
- Packham (5): knows how. Refuses. This score represents knowledge, not
  capacity. He is making a point.
- Irwin (45): actively against this as a philosophy. He was going to use
  that spear properly and not on a fish.
- Attenborough (0): would not.

### Domain 6 — Animal Encounters

**What it covers:** Behaviour identification, threat assessment, de-escalation,
safe handling (or safe non-handling). Species-specific knowledge. How to be
near a dangerous animal and survive. This is the panel's richest domain.

**Panel authorities:** O'Shea (99) / Stevens (99) → Irwin (97) → Attenborough (95)
→ Backshall (88) → Packham (85) → Cody (82) → Hales (80)

**Generation notes:**
- The top 3 have the most knowledge and the most bites. This is not
  coincidental. The knowledge does not prevent the bites.
- O'Shea and Stevens are joint highest. O'Shea's snake deviated from
  the published literature. Stevens's snake made an error of judgement.
  The snake's position is unrecorded.
- Irwin (97): the highest earned score. Every point is real and has
  a story attached.
- Attenborough (95): has observed more animal behaviour than anyone alive.
  Just won't intervene. This is technically a different skill but the
  knowledge is unmatched.
- Bear (68): confident about animals. Not always correctly. The panel knows
  this and says nothing because it would take too long.
- Fox (65): "is it a threat? No? Then it's fine." Functional, unromantic.
  The panel finds this simultaneously reasonable and insufficient.
- Darwin: crossed this domain's most dangerous territory with a specimen jar
  and an appetite.

### Domain 7 — Navigation

**What it covers:** Terrain reading, celestial navigation, dead reckoning,
map and compass, GPS, tracking your own route. Finding where you are and
getting somewhere else.

**Panel authorities:** Fox (96) → Ray (90) → Cody (88) → Stroud (85) / Hales (85)

**Generation notes:**
- Fox (96): SBS navigation is in a different category. Night, underwater,
  hostile environment, under fire. The panel's survival navigation problems
  are comparatively relaxed.
- Ray (90): terrain reading is fundamental to bushcraft. He reads landscapes
  the way other people read text.
- Attenborough (10): has been to many places. Was driven to all of them.
- Stevens (40) / O'Shea (40): gets to where the target species is.
  That's the extent of the navigation requirement.
- Darwin (65): five-year Beagle voyage. Managed fine. The ship had sailors.

### Domain 8 — Terrain & Weather

**What it covers:** Reading environmental conditions — storm prediction,
flash flood risk, avalanche terrain, desert heat management, jungle humidity.
Understanding what the landscape is telling you before it becomes critical.

**Panel authorities:** Ray (93) → Fox (92) → Cody (90) → Hales (88) / Stroud (88)

**Generation notes:**
- Ray (93): environmental reading is the intellectual core of bushcraft.
  He considers the landscape a text.
- Fox (92): tactical terrain reading — cover, approach routes, lines of
  sight. Different emphasis, same underlying skill.
- Attenborough (30): knows what things are. Cannot deploy this information
  practically. The gap between his knowledge and his practical survival
  capacity is the most extreme on the panel.
- Stevens / O'Shea: strong on snake habitat, limited elsewhere.

### Domain 9 — Tool-making & Kit

**What it covers:** Making tools from available materials — cordage, blades,
containers, shelters, traps. Also: what kit to carry, how to use it, how to
improvise when it's gone.

**Panel authorities:** Ray (98) → Cody (95) → Fox (88) → Hales (82)

**Generation notes:**
- Ray (98): will spend forty minutes making a friction fire kit from scratch
  and consider it time well spent. This is correct.
- Cody (95): his school's entire purpose. Primitive tools only. The multi-tool
  is a philosophical failing.
- Stroud (78): one multi-tool. That's it. Cody has looked at this multi-tool.
  He has not said anything. The look was sufficient.
- Bear (65): buys gear. Bear buys gear. He has good gear. He did not make it.
- Packham (25): has better things to think about.
- Irwin (35) / Attenborough (0): tool-making is not the point.
- Darwin (45): 19th century field naturalist kit. Specimen jars. Adequate
  for the original purpose.

### Domain 10 — Psychology

**What it covers:** Mental resilience under pressure — decision-making in
crisis, staying functional when cold/hungry/lost, managing fear, group
dynamics under stress, knowing when to stop and when to push.

**Panel authorities:** Cody (95) / Hales (95) → Bear (92) → Fox (90) → Irwin (88)

**Generation notes:**
- Cody and Hales share the top score but arrive via entirely different routes.
  Cody: stillness as a practice. Hales: flat affect as a survival mechanism.
  Both are unflappable. Both would rate each other highly without saying so.
- Bear (92): won't panic, even when he probably should. This is less a
  virtue and more an absence of an appropriate threat-response mechanism.
  Still useful in a survival situation.
- Fox (90): trained for pressure specifically. Baffled by peacetime feelings.
- Stevens (72): unshakeable self-belief. This is not the same as psychological
  health. The distinction matters for generation purposes.
- Darwin (68): patient, curious, occasionally overwhelmed by the modern panel.
  The panel is a lot.

### Domain 11 — Endurance

**What it covers:** Physical and mental capacity to keep going — cold, wet,
hungry, exhausted, no sleep. How long can you function and at what degraded
capacity?

**Panel authorities:** Fox (97) → Bear (95) → Cody (93) → Stroud (90) / Hales (90)

**Generation notes:**
- Fox (97): SBS selection is considered the hardest military selection in
  the world. This is the authority score for endurance.
- Bear (95): the genuine part. The hotel story is irrelevant to the physical
  capacity. He is extraordinary at keeping going.
- Attenborough (5): 97 years old. He is here. That is something.
- Packham (50): would rather not, but will.
- Darwin (60): five years on the Beagle. Seasick for much of it. Endured.
  The cook helped.

---

### Domain 12 — First Aid

**What it covers:** Wilderness-specific first aid — wound management, fracture
treatment, snakebite, hypothermia, dehydration, improvised stretchers,
emergency field surgery. Keeping someone alive until evacuation.

**Panel authorities:** Fox (88) → Cody (85) → Hales (80) → O'Shea (78) / Backshall (72)

**Generation notes:**
- Fox (88): combat first aid. The stakes were different. The principles apply.
- O'Shea (78) and Stevens (68): know snakebite from the inside. Both are
  on the WHO snakebite roster by lived experience if nothing else.
- Darwin (30): 19th century medicine. Do not let Darwin administer first aid.
  This is load-bearing guidance, not a joke.
- Attenborough (0): there is a crew member for this.

## Panel Roles by Domain Profile

**The Bushcraft Core (high across all survival domains):**
Ray, Cody, Hales, Stroud — the four who could actually keep you alive.
Between them they cover every domain except Animal Encounters at 90+.

**The Specialists (one or two domains dominant, others weak):**
O'Shea + Stevens: Animal Encounters 99, everything else poor.
Fox: Navigation 96, Endurance 97, Terrain 92 — military pattern, not wilderness.
Irwin: Animal Encounters 97, everything else secondary.
Darwin: Plant Knowledge 75, Animal Encounters 78, everything survival-critical low.

**The Engaged Non-Practitioners (knowledge without application):**
Attenborough: Animal Encounters 95, everything practical 0-30.
Packham: Plant 75, Animal 85, refuses Hunting, won't build anything.

**The Confident Generalist:**
Bear: Psychology 92, Endurance 95 genuinely earned. Everything else
serviceable but overstated in his own estimation.

**The Earnest All-Rounder:**
Backshall: no highs, no catastrophic lows. Competent across everything.
The most useful person on the panel if you're not looking for drama.
