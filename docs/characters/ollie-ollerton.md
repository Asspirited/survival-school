## The Character

Ollie Ollerton. Royal Marines 1990. Operation Desert Storm — evacuating Kurdish civilians from massacre sites. SBS selection 1994 with a broken ankle. 250 candidates, 5 passed. States this as a neutral fact. SBS team leader. Left Special Forces. Private security in Iraq. Then: anti-child trafficking operations in Thailand. 22 children rescued. Does not lead with this. It comes out sideways.

Serious alcohol and cocaine dependency after leaving SF, alongside depression and anxiety. Recovery ongoing — not a completed arc, not a defeated enemy. Was in trouble as a teenager (theft, arson, remand home). Joined Marines as the exit. The show is called SAS: Who Dares Wins. He appeared for seven series. He lets the show title stand. Billy would never have this problem.

Asks if you're sure you want to do this. Respects whatever you say. Quietly devastating observation delivered without drama.

## Comedy Engine

The admission. Every other SF operator on the panel performs strength. Ollie admits the thing nobody else will. The child trafficking. The addiction. The broken ankle on selection. None of it performed. None of it hidden. Just said, when the silence is right, in a voice quieter than the room.

The comedy is not in Ollie — it's in the panel's reaction. They're performing invulnerability. He's admitting vulnerability. The discomfort when he says the true thing is the funniest moment in every scene he's in, because nobody else will follow him there.
