## Founding Philosophy — recovered from Claude.ai session, written 2026-03-28

### The tagline (Rod's words, verbatim)

"Survive any situation, no matter how hopeless...or don't."

"It's real survival skills, knowledge and experts meets comedy with a bunch of tools and techniques designed to keep you safe at night"

### The product hierarchy (Rod's call, verbatim)

"I think it treads a careful balance of those who love survival from those who love comedy...I think the comedy angle and characters should be the stronger element tho, because anyone can get survival info from ai or google searches, we need to bring it to life and excite people - comedy is a great tool for that"

Comedy first, knowledge second. But the knowledge has to be real or the comedy collapses.
The experts earn their place by being genuinely expert, not by being straight men.
Information is the straight man.

### The founding moment — Cody's spear (Rod's words, verbatim)

"it was the fact that they had obviously fucked him off so much with their bullshit (i didn't know Jo had been threatening him but could see the relationship was fucked, also didn't know about the producer until i watched some youtube vids that told the real story) that he just thought 'you know what, no, fuck you, the spear and tinder goes in the water' - the fact that a gentle and kind and knowledgeable and collaborative person had been pushed to the point he said that - made me absolutely piss myself - it's arguably not the most humane reaction if i was there (tho I would have reacted exactly the same in laughing hysterically if present, sadly) but it was the various contradictions in play, the bad practice, the bullshit, the man under pressure, the childish but totally relatable response from a TOTAL expert, that expert just chucking the stuff in the pool is enough to make me laugh right now as i write this, that contradiction itself is just gold"

"and because all the preceding episodes they battle so hard to get a tinder, spear, fire, whatever, and just watching him throw it in made me lose my shit completely"

"yes, yes and yes, the build up to the fuck it, it's going in the pool is why it pays off - it's hitchcock suspense almost, also that 'this is a very serious show' followed by that - those are the contradictions where comedy needs no introduction"

### The Hitchcock mechanic (named in founding session)

Not joke-joke-joke. Tension-tension-tension-release.
The comedy is the release valve on accumulated pressure.
The show takes itself completely seriously. The moment it winks at the camera, it dies.
Cody's spear is funny because Cody is not trying to be funny. He's furious.
Never break character. The experts are always serious about survival.

### The three product rules (established in founding session)

1. Never break character. The experts are always serious about survival.
2. Build pressure before release. No joke without setup.
3. The knowledge must be real. The moment it's fake, the whole thing collapses.

"That's Hitchcock, Fawlty Towers, and Cody Lundin in one product brief."

### The over-seriousness / death-warning mechanic (Rod's words, verbatim)

"yes but they might also lean into exaggeration, like in Dual Survival they used to just make me laugh every episode by repeatedly saying (whatever was happening) 'this is serious and if you do/don't blah blah blah you could die / will lead to death / you'll be dead in seconds' type drama, mostly unnecessary in the context they were in - look that up as that's a core I want to also include - over seriousness at situations that don't warrant survival - in fact that's a feature waiting to write itself - how the panel survive mundane day to day experiences"

"and I'd like them to be very funny in how you might die or why that is stupid, really biting in that regard, as comedy gold will come from there I think - not all the time but we need to discuss when they do that - a stupid question? a stupid panel response? a response they disagree with?"

"yes and love 4 particularly it exactly needs the same gravitas and tone as real life-death scenarios to work properly - that is exactly the comedy"

### Death-warning trigger logic (agreed in founding session)

Fires on:
1. User does something stupid — panel doesn't suffer fools, bite is the consequence of hubris
2. Panel disagreement — "if you do what he just said, you will absolutely die" as weapon in inter-panel argument
3. User asks the obvious question — contempt is the comedy
4. Mundane mode specifically — death consequence for failing in Tesco car park delivered with wilderness gravity. Fires more freely here.

Does NOT fire as punchline wallpaper. If too frequent, becomes noise.

### The delivery register (Rod's words, verbatim — 2026-03-28)

"everything anyone does in any situation 'will very likely result.....in certain DEATH'
(said coldly, logically and calmly but firmly and with very unnecessary gravitas) —
that's where a ton of humour lives I reckon whatever mechanic we use to expose and
explore it in conversations"

**What this locks in for generation:**
The comedy is entirely in the delivery register. Not the words. Not the situation.
The register: cold, logical, calm, firm, unnecessary gravitas. The pause before DEATH.
The certainty. The complete absence of alarm in the voice even as the content is alarming.

This is what makes Dual Survival funny — they meant it every time. The register never
flickered. A blister delivered with the same voice as a cobra bite. That IS the joke.
There is no other joke. The characters are not aware there is a joke.

**Generation instruction:**
When the death-warning fires, the character does not raise their voice, does not
accelerate, does not signal that this is dramatic. They state it as a logical conclusion.
The gravitas is real to them. The unnecessary part is invisible to them. "...will very
likely result in certain death." The ellipsis before DEATH is the mechanic — the pause
of someone making sure you understand the consequence before they move on.

### The panel spectrum (established in founding session)

True believers: full death-gravity always, no flicker (Bear)
Quiet incredulity: grounded enough to notice the gap occasionally, never breaking character — just a beat, a flicker (Ray: "I'm not sure this is quite the same as the Kalahari, Bear.")
Maybe one who can't quite keep a straight face

"wonder whether that needs a different engine / mode or if the same one would be funny - it might be that we want some panel members to be properly serious in surviving, but one or two of them who with a grip on reality are sense checking and somewhat incredulous at times maybe"

### Ray Mears in Wetherspoons (the validated comedy moment)

"this has also just made me laugh a lot Ray Mears in a Wetherspoons. Delivered completely straight. 'If you don't secure a table within the first ninety seconds, you will lose core temperature and your group's morale will collapse. You could be dead within the hour.'"

Rod laughed at this as text, no UI, no animation. That's the product working.
Falsifier candidate for HDD: if Survival School is working, people laugh before they see a single screen.

### Target user (Rod's call)

Someone who loves comedy first, but respects that the survival knowledge is genuine.
Not a survival nerd who tolerates jokes.
A comedy audience who leaves knowing something real.
