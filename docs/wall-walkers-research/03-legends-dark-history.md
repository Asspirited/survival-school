# Legends, Dark History, Ghosts & Weird

## Roman Ghosts
- Treasurer's House York (1953): Harry Martindale saw Roman soldiers marching through cellar, visible only from knees up (floor level = original Roman road). Described exhausted auxiliary troops with round shields — detail confirmed before Martindale could have known.
- Milecastle 42 "Lucius the Sentry": repeated daytime apparition, so frequent he has a name
- Housesteads: nighttime ghostly legionnaires, partly transparent
- Phantom marching sounds: hobnailed sandals on stone, distant commands, especially in fog
- Brocolitia (Carrawburgh) temple site: phantom patrol reports
- Chesters: ghostly hoofbeats near river crossing

## The Hexham Heads & Werewolf (1971-72)
- Two boys (Colin & Leslie Robson) found stone heads in garden, Rede Avenue, Hexham
- Objects moved, bottles thrown. Heads turned on their own.
- Neighbour Nelly Dodd: half-man half-goat figure leaving her house
- Dr Anne Ross (Celtic scholar, Newcastle Uni): took heads for study. Woke to dark animal-like figure in doorway, rushed past her downstairs. Daughter saw werewolf on stairs.
- Previous resident Desmond Craigie claimed he made them from concrete in 1956 as toys
- Dr Ross and others disputed this — appeared genuinely ancient
- The heads have since VANISHED from public record

## The Border Reivers (c.1300-1603)
- Families who lived by raiding: Armstrong, Elliot, Graham, Nixon, Kerr, Robson, Charlton
- 300 years of cattle theft, murder, kidnap, arson, blackmail
- Gave us "bereaved" (reived = robbed) and "blackmail" (black mail = protection money)
- Armstrongs could field 3,000 men. Johnnie Armstrong hanged by James V despite safe conduct.
- Kerrs were left-handed — spiral staircases turned opposite way. "Ker-handed" = left-handed.
- The Hot Trod: pursue thieves across border with burning peat on lance
- Reiver's Moon: full moon that lit night raids (autumn, when cattle were fat)
- Debatable Lands: lawless no-man's-land between England and Scotland
- "Hob the King" (Halbert Elliot, 1580-90): murdered 14 people, burned homes with people inside
- Baunchling: ritual humiliation, glove on lance as "false hand"
- Busy Gap Rogues: named gang operating from Housesteads ruins
- Ended 1603 when James VI/I united crowns. Mass hangings, deportations to Ireland.

## The Cursing Stone
- Archbishop Gavin Dunbar of Glasgow, 1525: 1,069-word curse against Reivers
- Read from every pulpit in the Borders
- 2001: artist Gordon Young carved 14-ton granite boulder with full text, installed in Carlisle
- Since installation: flooding (2005, 2015), foot-and-mouth, factory closures, Carlisle United relegation
- Multiple councillors demanded removal. Councillor Jim Tootle blamed it for everything.
- Artist compared removal to Taliban destroying Bamiyan Buddhas

## The Solway Spaceman (1964)
- Jim Templeton photographed daughter near Burgh Marsh (close to Bowness)
- White spacesuit figure appeared behind her in developed photo
- Nobody else present. Kodak confirmed no tampering. MoD reportedly visited.
- Later tentatively attributed to overexposed wife in background — believers reject this
- "Cumberland Spaceman" remains unexplained

## Big Cat Sightings
- "Beast of Cumbria": large black panther-like cat near Stainton (2009)
- DNA confirmation (2023): Sharon Larkin-Snowden found black animal size of German shepherd on sheep carcass. University of Warwick analysis: Panthera genus (leopard or jaguar). ACTUAL SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE.
- ~80% sightings resemble black leopards, ~15% puma/cougar. ~31 reports in decade.

## Beast of Bolam Lake (2002-03)
- Late-night fishing party: 8-foot creature with glowing red eyes, standing upright
- Centre for Fortean Zoology: 19-person investigation team
- Conclusion: more "haunting or genius loci" than flesh-and-blood

## Renwick Cockatrice (c.1733)
- Church demolished → great beast with scaly body and leathery wings flew out
- John Tallentire killed it with a rowan bough
- His family exempted from church tithes FOREVER

## The Long Pack
- Pedlar arrives at remote house with heavy pack. Servants suspicious. Pack moves.
- They stab the pack. Blood flows out. Armed man inside, planning to let in robbers.
- Told since at least 18th century. Multiple variants. Sometimes presented as fact.

## The Lambton Worm
- John Lambton fished on a Sunday instead of going to church
- Caught small ugly worm, threw it down a well
- Went on Crusade. Worm grew enormous, wrapped around Penshaw Hill, ate livestock and children
- Cut in half → simply rejoins. Fought in River Wear wearing spike-covered armour.
- Curse: must kill first living thing after slaying worm. Father runs out. Can't kill father.
- Nine generations of Lambtons died violent deaths. Lambton family is real. Deaths verifiable.
- Geordie folk song still sung: "Whisht, lads, haad yer gobs..."

## Winter's Gibbet (1791)
- William Winter murdered Margaret Crozier near Elsdon
- At execution: carried both female accomplices from dock while supporting own chains AND theirs
- Body hung on gibbet until rotted. Smell so bad horses refused to pass.
- Replica gibbet with mannequin head still stands. HEAD KEEPS GETTING STOLEN.

## Alnwick Castle Vampire (12th century)
- William of Newburgh chronicle. Criminal died before confessing sins after catching wife cheating.
- Rose from tomb to prowl streets. Stench caused plague.
- Townspeople dug him up, dismembered, burned. Plague stopped.
- Predates Dracula by centuries. Among earliest English vampire accounts.

## Newcastle Witch Trials (1649-50)
- Scottish "witch pricker" hired at 20 shillings per guilty witch
- Method: stab with bodkin pin. If no bleeding/crying out = guilty
- 14 women and 1 man hanged on single day (August 1650) — largest mass witch execution in England
- Lt-Col Hobson grew suspicious of methods. Pricker fled courthouse.

## Chillingham Castle
- "Most haunted castle in England" (near Wall route)
- Blue Boy: child bricked up in wall, skeleton found during renovations
- Ghost of Lady Mary Berkeley
- White Pantry Ghost
- Genuine torture chamber with iron maiden, spiked chair
- Runs ghost tours

## Northumberland Folklore
- Redcaps: short goblin creatures with talons, red caps. Lurk in ruined castles.
- Gytrash/Barguest: giant ghostly dogs, eyes like burning coals, lead travellers astray
- Brown Man of the Moors: dwarf-like nature spirit, punishes hunters
- Silkies: female ghosts heard rustling in silk
- Cauld Lad of Hylton: ghost of murdered stable boy
