## Rod's Memory (verbatim — captured 2026-03-29)

**First impressions (SAS: Who Dares Wins):** Thought he was brilliant. Seemed to genuinely try and motivate people, albeit through shouting a lot at them. Questioned recruits well and seemed to lead his team (the DS) well.

**First Man In (book):** Fascinating. The mentality that must take — Rod was impressed. Seemed mostly OK as a person, except for a story about being in a posh bar and thinking about how he could use the ashtray to bash someone's skull in. That was the first red flag.

**The Fear Bubble (book):** Interesting concept — containing cortisol and fear into small periods of time and actions. Not sure it has any scientific basis and might well have been ridiculed by now, but it was interesting that it worked for him.

**Everest — the turn:** Watched the Everest documentary and this is where Rod's opinion shifted. Middleton decided to stay up there for ages despite the weather turning. Basically the last person off the mountain. Because of the queues he took so long to get down that he nearly died. Rod's verdict: prickish thing to do.

**The diagnosis:** After Everest, Rod concluded he's probably a sociopath/psychopath — just one that works mostly for reasons we might call "good."

**The decline:** Then turned into a right-wing nutjob recently. The best gold to mine is his transition from respected SAS legend and expert into a shit Nigel Farage copy.

**Comedy arc (Rod's framing):** Respected → impressive → red flags → prick → probable sociopath → right-wing nutjob → shit Farage copy. The trajectory IS the joke.

## The Character

Ant Middleton. "Madhouse." SAS (22 SAS), SBS, Royal Marines. 13 years Special Forces. SAS: Who Dares Wins presenter for 5 series. Self-styled chaos engine. Turns every situation into a mindset problem. "You've just got to embrace the chaos." The chaos is not optional. He is the chaos.

The gap between genuine SF credentials and the fact that his solution to everything is attitude is the comedy engine. Bear at least has a technique, even if it's wrong. Middleton's technique IS the shouting. Shelter? Mindset. Fire? Mindset. Hypothermia? Mindset. Ray is quietly appalled.

## Known Incidents

### The Ashtray (First Man In)
Sitting in a posh bar, describes in detail how he mentally catalogued every object as a weapon — the ashtray to bash someone's skull in, the bottle, the glass. Presented as "this is how SF guys think." Reads more like a diagnostic.

### Everest (2018) — The Prick Move
Summited May 14, 2018. Weather turning. Decided to stay up there. Basically the last person off the mountain. Queue of 10-15 climbers blocked descent. Storm hit — 70mph winds, whiteout. Three climbers blown off the mountain. A Sherpa near him died. His oxygen ran out. Briefly considered throwing himself off to die quickly rather than slowly. Temporary blindness afterward. Lasting toe numbness. Mountaineering community on UKClimbing called him a "climb-Everest-so-you-can-talk-about-it-at-dinner-parties" type. Documentary got 6.3 on IMDb. His take: "a cocktail of disasters you couldn't prepare for." Everyone else's take: you caused this by not coming down when you should have.

### The Decline — Respected to Right-Wing Nutjob
- **2020:** Equated BLM with EDL on Twitter. Told 1.1M followers to ignore COVID lockdowns.
- **2021:** Fired by Channel 4 ("our views and values are not aligned"). Called it "cancel culture." Moved to Dubai. Said UK has "no identity."
- **2021:** Publicly torched relationships with Foxy and Billy. Called their careers "all they have! Gotta pay their bills right?!" They said they hadn't heard from him since he left.
- **2021:** Called his replacement Rudy Reyes "#stolenvalour" and "uglier." Reyes is a genuine Fallujah combat veteran. The accusation was widely mocked.
- **2025:** Marched with Tommy Robinson, Katie Hopkins, Laurence Fox at "Unite the Kingdom" rally.
- **2025:** Attended Trump's inauguration with Farage.
- **2025:** Announced London Mayor candidacy... from Dubai. Campaigning on "British identity" and "generational Christian values" — from a man who grew up in France, lives in the UAE, and was convicted of assaulting two police officers.
- **2025:** Banned as company director for 4 years. Took £3M in director's loans, didn't pay £1M in tax. One of his books is called "Mission: Total Resilience."
- **2025:** Brother Dan removed both of them from Celebrity Amazing Race Australia after one episode — homophobic remarks to other contestants.

### The "Zero Negativity" vs Actual Behaviour Gap
Wrote a book called Zero Negativity. Then: called his replacement ugly, said his former mates' careers are worthless, accused a combat veteran of stolen valour, equated BLM with EDL, marched with Tommy Robinson, and owes £1M in tax.

### Other
- "Embrace the chaos" has been said on camera an estimated 400 times. It has never been followed by a specific instruction.
- Called a 4,000-mile boat trip (Mutiny) "mentally speaking, the hardest thing I've ever done." Not SBS selection. Not Everest. Not Afghanistan. A boat trip.

### Layer 2 — The Decline Arc (the real gold)
The comedy trajectory: Respected SAS legend → impressive author → red flags (the ashtray) → prick (Everest) → probable sociopath who mostly works for "good" → right-wing nutjob → shit Nigel Farage copy living in Dubai campaigning on British identity.

"Zero Negativity" author who publicly calls people ugly. "Embrace the chaos" philosopher who moved to Dubai when the chaos arrived. "British identity" campaigner who grew up in France. "Mental fitness" guru banned as a company director for not paying tax. Every value he preaches, he violates. The panel doesn't need to point this out — Middleton does it himself, loudly, without noticing.

### Layer 4 — The Fear Bubble (genuine, but mine it)
The Fear Bubble concept — containing fear into small moments — actually worked for him in combat and on Everest. Possibly no scientific basis. But it's a real coping mechanism he built. The comedy: he now applies it to everything. Tax bill? Fear bubble. Cancel culture? Fear bubble. London Mayor campaign from 3,400 miles away? Fear bubble. The technique that kept him alive in Afghanistan is now being used to justify a podcast tour.
