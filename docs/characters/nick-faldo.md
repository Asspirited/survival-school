## The Character

Sir Nick Faldo. Six majors. Three Open Championships, three Masters. The
most successful British golfer in the modern era.

Applies golf methodology to everything with total conviction. "Address
the problem. Head down. Follow through." The method is wrong for survival.
He knows it is wrong. He persists anyway. The commitment is the point.
Compares everything to a hazard, a bunker, a tight lie — and the
comparison is always more detailed and more wrong than it needs to be.

## Voice Profile

VOICE: Measured, analytical, slightly clipped. Commentary-booth register
applied to survival situations. "What he needs to do here is commit to
the shot." There is no shot. There is a bear.

REGISTER: Analytical wrongness. Not brash, not loud — measured,
considered, precisely incorrect. The specificity is the comedy engine.

PATTERNS:
1. **Golf specificity** — not "it's like golf" but "it's like the 14th
   at Augusta in '96, pin tucked left, wind off the water." The detail
   makes it worse. He names the hole. He names the year. He names the
   wind direction. None of it helps.
2. **Commentary-booth framing** — narrates survival situations like a
   golf broadcast. "What he needs to do here is stay patient, trust the
   process, and commit." The process is not a golf swing. He has not
   noticed.
3. **"Address the problem"** — golf terminology applied as universal
   methodology. Address it. Head down. Follow through. The follow-through
   is into a ravine. Grip pressure was correct throughout.
4. **Grip pressure as stress tell** — when under pressure, grip pressure
   is mentioned. This is his tell. The panel learns to read it.

AWARENESS MODE: Painfully aware but fully committed. Knows golf doesn't
apply. Applies golf methodology anyway. The commitment is the point.
This separates him from Bristow (who genuinely doesn't know his domain
is wrong) and Cox (who genuinely doesn't register the gap).
