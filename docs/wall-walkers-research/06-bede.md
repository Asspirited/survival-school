# The Venerable Bede — Research Summary

Full character doc at: docs/characters/venerable-bede.md
Full research at: cusslab/research/bede-character-research.md

## Key Facts
- Born c.672/3, died 26 May 735 (Ascension Day)
- Entered Monkwearmouth aged 7, transferred to Jarrow. Never left Northumbria.
- Wrote ~40 books from one monastic library
- Ecclesiastical History (731): ~160 surviving manuscripts
- Popularised AD dating system. First "Before Christ" equivalent.
- Corrected Pliny on tides using empirical observation
- Semi-sceptical about miracles — unusually careful for his era
- Half a bottle of wine per day (Benedictine Rule)
- Survived plague at 14 — sang psalms with one old man across empty monastery for weeks

## What He Got Wrong
- Attributed Hadrian's Wall to SEVERUS, not Hadrian
- Working from Gildas (unreliable) and Orosius (Roman propagandist)
- Never measured the Wall himself — "eight feet in breadth and twelve in height" (close but wrong)
- Heavy Christian interpretation — everything is God's plan
- Northumbrian bias — favoured his own kingdom
- Called Picts/Scots "worms" — not neutral journalism
- Monk from age 7 writing about warfare, politics, kings he never met
- Miracle selection = cherry-picking God's greatest hits

## Death Scene
- Ill for weeks, continued dictating translation of St John's Gospel
- Boy scribe Wilberht: "There is still one sentence."
- Bede: "Quick, write it down." Pause. "There; now it is written." "Good. It is finished."
- Lay on floor facing his altar. Sang Glory Be. Died.

## Myths
- Angel inscribed "VENERABILIS" on tombstone overnight — no early authority
- Bones stolen by monk Alfred Westou (~1020), hidden in St Cuthbert's coffin
- Rediscovered 1104, eventually given own shrine at Durham Cathedral
