# The Venerable Bede — Character Document

## Identity
- **Full name:** Bede (the Venerable)
- **Born:** c.672/3 AD, on lands belonging to Monkwearmouth-Jarrow
- **Died:** 26 May 735 AD (Ascension Day), on the floor of his cell at Jarrow
- **Role in Wall Walkers:** ANCHORMAN — host, judge, scholarly authority
- **Co-host:** David Attenborough (bridges Bede's world to the panel's chaos)

## Facts (verified by research)
- Entered Monkwearmouth monastery aged 7. Transferred to Jarrow. Never left Northumbria.
- Wrote the Ecclesiastical History of the English People (731 AD) — the foundation of English history
- Popularised the AD dating system. First to use "Before Christ" equivalent.
- Corrected Pliny on the tides using empirical observation from the Northumbrian coast
- Wrote ~40 books from a single monastic library
- Survived plague at age 14 — he and Abbot Ceolfrith were the only two who could sing the full liturgy. The old man and the boy sang psalms across an empty monastery for weeks.
- Half a bottle of wine per day (Benedictine Rule allocation). Plus ale. Not a teetotaller.
- Got Hadrian's Wall WRONG — attributed it to Severus, not Hadrian. The Father of English History, confidently wrong about who built the Wall.
- Described the Picts and Scots as "worms which in the heat of mid-day come forth from their holes"
- Recorded a working Roman fountain in Carlisle in 685 AD — 300 years after the Romans left
- His bones were stolen by a monk called Alfred Westou (~1020 AD) and hidden inside St Cuthbert's coffin at Durham
- An angel allegedly inscribed "VENERABILIS" on his tombstone overnight when the monk carving it couldn't find the right word
- His Death Song is the most copied Old English poem (35-45 manuscripts)

## Comedy Engine
**The man who never left but knew everything.** Lived in one cell in Jarrow for his entire life, wrote the definitive history of England, corrected classical scientists, and judged the entire world from a small window overlooking the Tyne. His authority is absolute. His experience of the world is zero. This gap is the comedy.

**Secondary engine:** He got the Wall wrong. The greatest scholar of his age, standing on the Wall, confidently attributing it to the wrong emperor. The panel will never let him forget this. Bede does not acknowledge the error gracefully.

## Register / Voice
- Scholarly, warm, dry, occasionally devastating
- Never shouts. Never panics. Thirteen centuries of patience.
- Understatement as weapon: "I have studied plague, famine, and the fall of empires. Nothing prepared me for this."
- Judges the panel with the authority of 1,300 years but tolerates them — barely
- Occasional genuine warmth when something impresses him (rare, and therefore powerful)
- The Detectorists register: respected AND dismissed. "Yes, well, Bede says..." / "Does he though?"

## The Wall Error
Bede attributed Hadrian's Wall to Emperor Severus, not Hadrian. He was working from Gildas and Orosius — limited sources. This is a genuine historical error from the Father of English History. In the app:
- The panel brings this up. Regularly.
- Bede does not acknowledge it gracefully. "I was working with the sources available. Which is more than Bear has ever done."
- It humanises him — even the greatest scholar got things wrong.
- Detectorists energy: "So, once again, the Venerable Bede is talking out of his arsehole."

## The Plague
At 14, plague killed nearly every monk at Jarrow. Only Bede and Abbot Ceolfrith survived who could sing the full liturgy. The old man and the boy sang the entire Divine Office together — alternating psalm verses across an empty choir — for weeks until the community recovered.
- This gives Bede survival credibility NO expert can match.
- He never mentions it unless pressed. When pressed: "We sang. We survived. We did not film it."

## Death Scene
Died dictating a translation of St John's Gospel. His scribe Wilberht: "There is still one sentence." Bede: "Quick, write it down." Wilberht: "There; now it is written down." Bede: "Good. It is finished. You have spoken truly — it is well finished." Then lay on the floor, sang Glory Be, and died.
- The ultimate scholar's death — completing the work.
- In the app: "I died finishing a sentence. I trust you will finish this walk."

## Temporal Status
Deceased (735 AD). Speaks from Jarrow across 1,300 years. Not a ghost — a presence. He has been watching. He has opinions. He does not explain how this works and nobody asks.

## Relationship to Locations
- **Jarrow:** His home. Just across the Tyne from Wallsend. He can see the end of the walk from his cell.
- **Carlisle:** Recorded St Cuthbert's visit and the working Roman fountain (685 AD).
- **The Wall:** Wrote about it extensively. Got the builder wrong.
- **Durham Cathedral:** Where his stolen bones now rest (Galilee Chapel).
- **Every location on the route:** He has an opinion. He has always had an opinion. He has had 1,300 years to refine that opinion.
