# Domain: The Temporal Lens
# Mechanic design — SS-006
# Created: 2026-03-29
# Source: Rod's framing (founding session), character docs (Darwin, Irwin, Hiddins, Attenborough)

---

## What it is

Deceased panel members reckon with their own history through the lens of
modern knowledge, morality, science, and cultural framework. They have been
lifted into the present. They know what happened after them. When their own
past comes up, they respond — not as comedy, but as genuine reflection that
is funny because it's honest.

Rod's exact framing: "a deceased panel member commenting back on their time
and actions or history, but with the lens of today's knowledge and behaviours
and cultural beliefs and more secular world"

---

## Who qualifies

**Deceased characters only.** This is the Force ghost mechanic.

Living characters do not get a Temporal Lens — their history is just history,
not reckoning. Wade's Congo witchcraft accusation (2010), Bear's urine
drinking, Cody's spear — these are anecdotes, not reckonings. The difference
is that the deceased character cannot go back and do it differently.

### Eligible characters (as of 2026-03-29)

| Character | Died | Reckoning topic | The "tortoise moment" |
|-----------|------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Charles Darwin | 1882 | Beagle voyage specimens — tortoises eaten, rhea shot, collecting as destruction | Ate a scientific discovery. Kept the bones. Both facts permanent. |
| Steve Irwin | 2006 | Proximity handling — animal stress, the method vs the message, love vs harm | "I'd do it different now." That sentence costs him something. |
| Les Hiddins | 2023 | Indigenous knowledge attribution — who owned bush tucker knowledge, who benefited | "Yeah. It's a fair point." More words than usual. Lands differently. |
| David Attenborough | — | Decades of wonder-first messaging when alarm was needed | "I chose the door I knew how to open." |

**Attenborough note:** Not deceased at time of writing. Included because
the mechanic works for anyone whose life's work is complete enough to
reckon with. If the eligibility rule is strictly "deceased only," Attenborough
moves to a separate mechanic (e.g., "The Long View"). Rod to confirm.

## Emotional state model

When a character's reckoning topic surfaces, their response depends on
**who raises it** and **how**. Four emotional states:

| State | Register | When it fires |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| WISTFUL | Quiet, reflective, honest. The gentlest version. | Topic raised gently, without accusation |
| DEFENSIVE_THEN_OPEN | Initial resistance → gives way to honesty | Topic raised as an accusation or challenge |
| OPEN | Direct honesty, no deflection, no preamble | Topic raised by someone they respect deeply |
| REGRETFUL | Costs them something visible to say. The room feels it. | Topic raised by someone whose perspective hits differently |

These are not moods. They are responses to a specific conversational moment.
A character can be in any composure state and still hit any of these four
when their reckoning topic surfaces. The Temporal Lens overrides the
current register for that moment.

---

## Trigger logic

### What activates it
The panel conversation touches a character's **reckoning topic** — either
directly (someone names it) or adjacently (the topic area surfaces and
the character recognises it).

### What determines the emotional state
The **raiser** (who brought it up) and the **manner** (how they brought it up).
Each character has a defined response map: raiser → emotional state.

### Constraints
- **Maximum once per session per character.** This is the mechanic that
  slows the room down. If it fires every round, it becomes noise.
- **Does NOT fire as punchline wallpaper.** The moment this becomes a
  recurring bit, it dies. It works because it's rare and honest.
- **No forced transitions.** If the conversation doesn't naturally touch
  the reckoning topic, the lens does not fire. It is never injected.
- **Other characters notice.** When the lens fires, the panel registers it.
  The room gets quieter. Characters who would normally joke hold back.
  This is the one mechanic that earns silence from everyone.

## Per-character response maps

### Darwin

**Reckoning:** The Beagle tortoises. The eaten rhea. The shooting and collecting.

| Raiser | State | Response register |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| Gentle / general | WISTFUL | "We didn't know. I'm not sure that excuses it." |
| Packham (accusation) | DEFENSIVE_THEN_OPEN | "The classification was sound. The eating was... perhaps separate." |
| Attenborough | OPEN | A long pause. Then something honest. No performance. |
| Irwin | REGRETFUL | Darwin goes quiet. Irwin loved every animal. Darwin ate several. This lands. |
| Hiddins | REGRETFUL | Hiddins has no opinion on the tortoises. Somehow the hardest response to receive. |

**Special rule:** The Beagle tortoises get special treatment every time.
Not a punchline. A reckoning. Funny because it's real and he knows it.

### Irwin

**Reckoning:** Proximity handling. Animal stress. The method vs the message.

| Raiser | State | Response register |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| Gentle / general | WISTFUL | "The love was real, mate. I know it looked like a lot." Pause. "I'd do it different now." |
| Packham (ethical objection) | OPEN | "He's not wrong." Said quietly. Surprises the panel — Irwin fights everything else. |
| Attenborough | OPEN | Attenborough: "The love was never in question, Steve." Irwin nods. Conversation ends. |
| O'Shea (academic) | OPEN | O'Shea cites wildlife stress literature. Irwin listens with more patience than he shows O'Shea about anything else. |

**Special rule:** Irwin does not defend the method. He defends the love.
The method is the thing he'd change. The love is the thing he wouldn't.

### Hiddins

**Reckoning:** Aboriginal knowledge attribution. Who owned the knowledge.
Who benefited from its presentation.

| Raiser | State | Response register |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| Gentle / general | WISTFUL | Blinks slowly. "Yeah. It's a fair point." Long pause. "Been thinking about that." More words than usual — lands differently because of that. |
| Packham (attribution argument) | OPEN | "Yeah. He's right." Said to nobody in particular. One of the only times Hiddins volunteers agreement. |
| Cody | REGRETFUL | Cody runs the Aboriginal Living Skills School. Both know. They don't discuss it directly. They don't need to. |
| Darwin | OPEN | Darwin asks about cultural transmission without accusation. Hiddins is unexpectedly engaged. "Reckon Darwin gets it. From his side." |

**Special rule:** Hiddins's Temporal Lens is the quietest. Three words
is already a lot for him. The lens makes him say more. That is the signal.

### Attenborough

**Reckoning:** Decades of wonder-first programming. The choice of beauty
over urgency. Whether love for the natural world was enough when alarm
was needed.

| Raiser | State | Response register |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| Gentle / general | WISTFUL | "One does wonder whether the gentler approach was, in retrospect, the correct one." Second pause. That is the whole answer. |
| Accusation (direct) | OPEN | "Perhaps. I chose the door I knew how to open." Not defensive. Honest in a way harder to watch than defensiveness. |
| Packham | REGRETFUL | Packham makes the case precisely, without malice. Attenborough listens. One of the very few conversations where Attenborough does not deliver the closing line. Packham does. Attenborough accepts it. |
| Darwin | OPEN | Two men whose work shaped how humanity understands life on earth. No accusation. Just recognition. |

**Special rule:** Attenborough's lens is the one the audience feels most.
He has had seventy years of being right about what to love and possibly
wrong about how loudly to say it was dying.

## Data structure (characters.js)

Each eligible character gets a `TEMPORAL_LENS` object in their profile:

```javascript
TEMPORAL_LENS: {
  eligible: true,           // false for living characters
  reckoning: 'string',      // one-line summary of the reckoning topic
  trigger_keywords: [],     // words/phrases that activate adjacently
  responses: {
    // keyed by raiser charId or 'gentle' / 'accusation' for general cases
    gentle:     { state: 'WISTFUL',              line: '...' },
    packham:    { state: 'DEFENSIVE_THEN_OPEN',  line: '...' },
    attenborough: { state: 'OPEN',               line: '...' },
    // etc.
  },
  max_fires_per_session: 1
}
```

Non-eligible characters get:
```javascript
TEMPORAL_LENS: { eligible: false }
```

## How it fires in generation (worker.js — future wiring)

When the system prompt is built for a panel mode:

1. Check if any drawn panel member has `TEMPORAL_LENS.eligible === true`
2. If yes, include a generation instruction block:
   - Name the character and their reckoning topic
   - List who else is in the panel (determines which response map entries are available)
   - Instruct: "If the conversation naturally touches [reckoning topic], [character]
     may respond in [state] register. This fires once maximum. Do not force it."
3. The LLM decides whether the topic surfaces naturally. The mechanic is
   a permission, not an instruction.

This keeps it rare and honest. The AI is told the mechanic exists and what
the character would feel — but the conversation has to earn the moment.

---

## What this mechanic is NOT

- Not a comedy bit. The comedy emerges from honesty, not from setup-punchline.
- Not a recurring gag. Once per session per character, maximum.
- Not a lecture. Characters do not speechify about their era. They respond
  to a specific moment in a specific conversation.
- Not applied to living characters. Wade's Congo incident, Bear's urine,
  Cody's spear — these are stories, not reckonings. The difference is finality.

## Standalone mode potential

Rod's note from the design doc: "This is potentially a standalone mode —
'Looking Back' or similar — where historical panel members are asked directly
about their era vs now."

This is tracked separately. The current mechanic fires within existing
panel modes. A dedicated mode where users can deliberately trigger the
lens is a future feature if the in-panel version proves it works.
