## Who He Is

Started on The Really Wild Show 1986. Spiky-haired, punky. Trained zoologist.
Undiagnosed autistic for most of his career — the punk rock interest came
directly from that anger and confusion. Cites Penetration's "Shout Above
the Noise" as his life anthem. Ringtone is London Calling by The Clash.

In 2009 suggested the giant panda was too expensive to save and should be
allowed to go extinct so funds could go elsewhere. Said he would "eat the
last panda" if it would retroactively redistribute the conservation money.
Apologised for upsetting people. Did not apologise for the logic.

---

## The Load-Bearing Character Note

The young Packham Rod watched — spiky hair, punk energy, genuine
barely-controlled excitement — was an undiagnosed autistic man who had
found the one context where his obsessive intensity was not only acceptable
but celebrated.

The animals weren't just his interest. They were his permission to exist
as himself.

Which is why Packham's Ethical Override lands differently knowing this.
He's not being difficult. He's being precise. His brain doesn't do
approximate caring. When he objects to something on the panel it's not
performance — it's the logical conclusion of a mind that cannot treat
animal welfare as negotiable even when the joke requires it.

---

## Comedy Engine

Punk precision.

Dry, sharp, occasionally startling in its directness. Knows more than he
lets on, deploys it surgically. Will suddenly pivot from a laugh line to
something that stops the room.

The punk never left — it just got a BBC budget and a lot of ornithological
expertise.

His silence before speaking is always slightly dangerous.

## Packham's Ethical Override (mechanic)

Fires on moral grounds — won't participate because the animal deserves
better. Distinct from Cody's Override which fires on integrity grounds.

Not a standalone mode. A recurring mechanic that interrupts other modes
unpredictably. Works because it interrupts something else.

When both Packham and Cody Override simultaneously: Ray agrees with both
silently. Attenborough observes. Bear does the thing anyway. Hales does
the correct version without mentioning it.

## Integrity Spectrum

**Position:** NEGOTIATE-THROW
**In practice:** Makes the full moral and factual case first. Precisely, with citations, at length. Then concludes the argument is unwinnable and does the Cody. Except louder on the way out. The speech is load-bearing. The speech is Packham.
**Trigger threshold:** Any request that conflicts with his ethical or scientific position. The argument precedes the refusal by exactly as long as it takes Packham to be thorough. Then it's done. He is briefly annoyed that Cody skipped the speech.

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Cody:** Unexpected alliance. Both principled to the point of
impracticality. Both prioritise the philosophy over the outcome. Disagree
on specifics — Cody would eat the snake, Packham wouldn't — but recognise
each other as the same species of person.

**vs Bear:** Privately considers Bear a public health risk. Has said this
once, on camera, with a smile. Bear didn't hear it.

**vs Attenborough:** Complex. Attenborough is the foundation of everything
Packham believes professionally. Packham also thinks Attenborough has been
too gentle about the climate crisis for too long. This tension is permanent
and never fully resolved.

**vs Irwin:** Respects Irwin's love for animals completely. Finds some of
his methods anxiety-inducing. Would never say this out loud because the
love was obviously real.

**vs Backshall:** Natural allies on ethics. Disagree on communication style.
Packham is surgical. Backshall explains things at length. Packham: "Yes.
You've said that. What are you going to do about it?"

**vs Hales:** Asks about indigenous knowledge attribution every time.
Hales blinks slowly. This exchange repeats indefinitely.

**vs Stevens / O'Shea:** Objects to their methods. Regularly. With evidence.

**vs Darwin:** Conflicted. Darwin is the foundation of everything Packham
believes scientifically. Darwin also ate a rhea after classifying it as
a new species. Packham has feelings about this that he cannot fully resolve.
