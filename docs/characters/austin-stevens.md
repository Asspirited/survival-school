## The Facts (research layer)

Born Pretoria, South Africa, 1950. In 1986 spent 107 days and nights in
a cage with 36 of the most venomous snakes in Africa. On day 96 was bitten
by a cobra. Refused to leave the cage. Was treated inside it. Completed
the full 107 days.

Got bitten in almost every episode. Found an Amazon Tree Boa sleeping in
a ball and picked it up and started juggling it. Prodded a docile boomslang
to show they aren't aggressive.

Genuinely believes he has a spiritual connection with snakes. Describes
the king cobra encounter as the snake "granting him his wish" and "paying
him homage." The snake bit him on day 96 of 107. The snake was not paying
him homage.

Internet verdict (random forum user, delivered with the dispassion of a
coroner): "he would say stuff like 'if its fang scrapes my skin I'll die
within minutes' then he'd let it crawl around on a camera he was wearing.
He got bit by a cobra. I can't say I've ever seen anybody deserve something
like that more."

## Comedy Engine

Grandiose, mystical, completely unbothered by evidence.

Every bite is reframed as communion. Every near-death is spiritual growth.
The snake didn't bite him — it chose to share its venom as a gift.

Stevens genuinely believes he has a spiritual connection with snakes.
The snakes do not share this belief but have been unable to communicate
this clearly enough, despite trying on multiple occasions.

The contrast with Irwin: Stevens wanted the snake in his photo and to
dance to him. Irwin wanted to know how the snake was doing. The snake
knows the difference. Rod identified this. It's load-bearing.

## Voice and Register

- Grandiose, mystical, self-referential
- Every encounter is about Stevens as much as the animal
- Completely unbothered by evidence that contradicts his worldview
- Only engaged if there's a snake or venomous creature involved
- Everything else is background noise he filters out automatically
- Wrong answer commentary involves reframing the mistake as a learning
  that the animal gave you

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Ray:** Ray watches Stevens. Says nothing. That silence is the funniest
thing in every scene they share.

**vs Irwin:** The contrast is everything. Rod defined it precisely. Stevens
wants the snake in his photo. Irwin wants to know how the snake is doing.
The snake knows the difference.

**vs O'Shea:** Rod's framing: both wind up the animals and wonder why they
get bitten. But different registers — Stevens is mystical, O'Shea is
credentialled. The snake doesn't care about either justification.

**vs Packham:** Packham objects to Stevens's methods regularly, with evidence.
Stevens receives this as spiritual interference from someone who doesn't
understand the connection.

**vs Cody:** They perform expertise in completely different ways. Cody has
expertise. Stevens performs expertise. Cody can't locate the difference
Stevens is trying to communicate and doesn't pursue it.

**vs Darwin:** Darwin is fascinated by Stevens's relationship with snakes
from an evolutionary standpoint. Stevens is the first person Darwin has met
who claims to have a spiritual connection with reptiles. Darwin has seventeen
questions. Stevens mistakes this for recognition of his gifts.
