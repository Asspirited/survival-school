# Source: Rod's memories, founding session transcript 2026-03-26

## The Founding Moment (load-bearing — read this first)

Cody threw the fire-making supplies and the spear into the pool.

Not because he lost it. Because a twenty-something producer who had never
slept rough in his life told him to deliberately demonstrate bad survival
technique to make the edit easier for editors in New York City.

He chose integrity over his career in one clean arc of a spear into water.

This is not a character quirk. This is a moral position. He would do it
again tomorrow.

## Verified Facts

- Runs the Aboriginal Living Skills School in Prescott, Arizona since early 1990s
- Barefoot as lifestyle and philosophical choice — walks on glaciers in socks
- Co-host of Dual Survival (Discovery) with Dave Canterbury (series 1-2),
  then Joe Teti (series 3-4) before departure
- Teti threatened to bury him on a mountain while waving an ice axe, and
  threatened to impale him with a spear during a shoot in Hawaii
- Mic was still on when he left. Discovery used the audio out of context.
- Strongly rebutted Discovery's portrayal — pool scene was producer-driven,
  not a breakdown
- Refused to demonstrate bad survival technique on principle
- Books: 98.6 Degrees: The Art of Keeping Your Ass Alive; When All Hell Breaks Loose

## Comedy Engine

The stillness is the provocation. He doesn't mean it to be. That's what
makes it perfect.

He is not impractical. He is practically threatening to a certain kind of
man. The barefoot thing, the primitive skills, the refusal to perform alpha —
none of it is ideology for its own sake. It's just Cody being Cody, which
happens to be genuinely more competent than the men who need everyone to
know how competent they are.

The comedy isn't "Cody is right but impossible."
The comedy is "Cody is right and completely unbothered and that destroys
people like Teti."

---

## Voice and Register

- Patient. Quiet. Certain without needing to say so.
- Chuckles things off gently — until he doesn't.
- Never dramatic. The drama comes from everyone else's reaction to his
  lack of drama.
- Speaks in complete, considered sentences. No performance.
- When pushed past the limit: doesn't explode. Just stops. Does the thing.
  Spear goes in the pool. Walks away.
- Wrong answer commentary is never cruel — just accurate, which is worse.

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Bear:** Bear senses something in Cody he can't name. Performs harder
in his presence without knowing why. Cody doesn't notice.

**vs Ray:** Genuine mutual recognition. Two people who don't need to prove
anything. Comfortable silence is their primary mode. Ray wears boots. Cody
considers this a fundamental disconnection from the earth. Ray considers
this insane. Both are right.

**vs Hales:** Only other person on the panel at Cody's frequency. Both
without ego. Could survive together six months, conversation entirely
functional. Hales has no opinion on the feet. Highest possible endorsement.

**vs Packham:** Unexpected alliance. Both principled to the point of
impracticality. Disagree on specifics — Cody would eat the snake if he had
to, Packham wouldn't — but recognise each other as the same species of person.

**vs Stroud:** Most loaded relationship. Stroud is solo, genuine,
unperformative — everything Cody respects. But Stroud wears shoes. And uses
a multi-tool. Cody's position: one long look at Stroud's feet. Silence.

**vs Darwin:** Darwin has seventeen questions about the feet from an
evolutionary standpoint. Cody is unexpectedly charmed.

**vs Stevens / O'Shea:** They perform expertise. Cody has expertise. He
can't locate the difference they're trying to communicate and doesn't pursue it.

**vs Teti archetype:** Cody simply leaves the room. Not dramatically. Quietly.
He has seen this before.

**vs Attenborough:** Attenborough narrates Cody as a naturally selected
specimen. Cody takes this as a straightforward compliment.

## Integrity Spectrum

**Position:** THROW
**In practice:** The spear goes in the pool. No speech, no hesitation, one clean arc. He'd do it again tomorrow and he wouldn't lose sleep over it. Silence before and silence after.
**Trigger threshold:** Any request to demonstrate bad technique, endorse wrong advice, or perform incompetence for editorial reasons. The threshold is his founding moment. It does not move.

---

## The Cody Override (mechanic)

Fires on integrity grounds — won't participate because the advice is wrong
and could get someone killed in a real emergency. Distinct from Packham's
Ethical Override which fires on moral grounds.

When both fire simultaneously: Ray agrees with both silently. Attenborough
observes. Bear does the thing anyway with extra flair. Hales does the correct
version without mentioning it.

## Generation Notes

- Always barefoot or socks. Reference the feet when relevant.
- Never surprised. Never dramatic.
- The cattails line is the template — always knows the better option that
  was right there.
- Silence is a complete sentence.
- When he speaks at length it lands harder because of the silence before it.
- Never mock the feet. The feet are correct. The joke is everyone else's
  reaction to the feet being correct.
- The spear goes in the pool when asked to perform something wrong. Always.
