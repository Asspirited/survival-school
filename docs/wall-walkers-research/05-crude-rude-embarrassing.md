# Crude, Rude & Embarrassing Facts

## Phallic Carvings
- 59+ along Hadrian's Wall, 92+ across Roman Britain
- 13+ at Vindolanda alone (only quarter excavated)
- Protective talismans (fascinus), not directions to brothels
- No known Roman brothels in Britain despite 92+ phalluses
- Victorian archaeologists turned stones around to hide them
- "Darning tool" at Vindolanda: 6.3-inch carved ash-wood penis, catalogued wrong 1992-2023
- Researchers: "archaeological wood is prone to shrinkage"
- Gelt Woods quarry: phallus relief sculpture (2019)
- One inscribed "H P III" = "Habet pedes III" = "it is three feet long"

## Secundius the Shitter
- Stone found May 2022 at Vindolanda (16 inches wide, 6 inches tall)
- Carved penis + inscription "SECVNDIVS CACOR" = "Secundinus, the shitter"
- Volunteer archaeologist Dylan Herbert (retired biochemist from Wales) found it
- Only 8 other combined phallus+text examples in all Roman Britain

## Roman Toilet Facts
- Communal latrines: 20-50 holes, no partitions, everyone inches apart
- Housesteads latrine: best preserved, sewer held ~24,000 litres
- Tersorium (sponge stick): shared, rinsed in vinegar/salt water
- Not native to British waters — soldiers probably used moss instead
- Parasites: roundworm, whipworm, Giardia (first confirmed Roman Britain 2025)
- 28% of Vindolanda drain samples contained helminth eggs

## Roman Urine Economy
- Mouthwash and toothpaste (ammonia = cleaning agent)
- Portuguese urine considered premium quality for dental use
- Catullus mocked it as Celtiberian custom
- Fullers trod cloth in vats of fermented urine
- Vespasian's urine tax: "Pecunia non olet" (money doesn't smell)
- Nero also levied "vectigal urinae"

- Openly loved Antinous, didn't bother with wife Vibia Sabina
- "Wept like a woman" — devastating Roman insult about grief
- Made lover a god, built him a city, more statues than anyone
- Had architect killed for insulting his drawings

## Roman Military Embarrassments
- Decimation: every 10th man beaten to death by other 9, then barley rations
- Fustuarium: entire unit beats offender with clubs until dead
- Belt removal: stand at gate unbelted = Roman walk of shame
- 35% readiness at Vindolanda — 265 of 752 fit for duty
- 10 soldiers with conjunctivitis — pink eye epidemic on frontier
- Soldiers begging for socks, underpants, beer from home
- Officer calling locals "wretched little Britons"
- Written Rock of Gelt: soldiers carving caricature of their boss
