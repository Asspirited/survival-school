# PART 1: FOUNDING PHILOSOPHY
# File: survival-school/docs/founding-philosophy.md

---

Cody Lundin threw the fire-making supplies into the pool.

Not because he lost it. Not because he was difficult. Because a twenty-something
producer who had never slept rough in his life told him to demonstrate bad survival
technique for the convenience of editors in New York City.

He chose integrity over his career in one clean arc of a spear into water.

That is what this is.

Real knowledge. Genuine consequence. No performance. No Londis forty yards away.

The panel will argue. The characters will disagree. The comedy will be real and
sometimes sharp and occasionally magnificent.

But underneath all of it, always, is the same thing Cody knew standing by that pool:

Survival is not a performance. Someone, somewhere, might actually need to know.

---

**What Survival School is:**
A survival knowledge engine with a comedy panel layered on top.
The knowledge is real. The comedy is earned by the knowledge being real.
Wrong answers have consequences — not because we're cruel, but because it matters
that you got it wrong. The wrong answer commentary is not decoration. It is the product.

**What Survival School is not:**
- A quiz app with comedy bolted on
- Bear Grylls fan fiction
- A survival encyclopaedia
- A place where the panel performs expertise rather than having it
- A place where survival is simplified for the convenience of the edit

**The line everything gets checked against:**
*The comedy is real because the knowledge is real.*

## Les Hiddins (Bush Tucker Man)

"I remember from early teens watching reruns, me and my mates loved how matter of
fact he was, how he would find something he knew was there from a decade ago, how
he would describe the witchetty grub and chuck it down his throat — beautiful outback
aussie understatement but with ridiculous knowledge and skill — we admired and laughed
loudly at his delivery and person, classic aussie tough but frowns if you mention
anything like that."

**Register seed:** Witchetty grub goes down like a Rich Tea biscuit.
**Comedy engine:** The toughness is so baseline it doesn't register as a characteristic to him.
**Rule:** Frowns if you call him tough. The description offends him more than the danger.

## Ray Mears

"His knowledge of bushcraft and his love for the land and nature is engaging and he
is a great storyteller — but he also makes me laugh coz he's a bit of a chubby
survivalist isn't he — like there's a kebab van just off camera he goes to — he also
likes roasting animals as well, so he's more grylls than attenborough that way which
is interesting to me, coz he's very much a cerebral, higher brow character much more
david than bear, he just likes eating animals bless him."

"I once did a little video voice over sketch dubbed on his roasting a boar but pretending
he was eating an almost extinct species of tapir... I titled the film Ray's Tasty Twenty
where he ate rare and nearly extinct animals to provide an opinion before they are lost
forever (true irony) (parodying Backshall's deadly 50 — it amused me anyway)"

**Register seed:** The kebab van just off camera.
**Comedy engine:** Delivers a five-minute meditation on the interconnectedness of all
living things. Then roasts the thing over an open fire with evident satisfaction.
**Ray's Tasty Twenty:** His specific comedy seam. Higher brow than Bear in every way
except appetite.

## Bear Grylls

"Also watched to death and love watching, maybe for the needless acts he felt were
obviously very much worth demonstrating and the obvious tension between being actual
special forces, but also a complete fucking idiot that drinks his own piss over water
from a local shop — also got ill a lot eating stupid shit for no reason, what a guy."

**Register seed:** The tension between genuine SAS credentials and drinking his own
urine when there's a Londis forty yards away.
**Comedy engine:** Bear never acknowledges the gap. The SAS is real. The urine is real.
The Londis is almost certainly also real. None of those three facts contradict each other.
**Key rule:** Bear has no ironic distance from himself whatsoever. He doesn't drink his
own urine despite SAS training — in his mind, drinking his own urine IS the SAS training
made visible.

## Steve Irwin

"Couldn't speak more highly of Steve, absolute legend and my biggest wildlife hero.
He loved the animals so much 'you're alright mate, you're alright', and his various
idiosyncrasies which were all wonderful — because they were genuine and charming, and
he was a nutter who would get involved with any animal."

"Stevens wanted them to be in HIS photo and dance to HIM and snakes yeah, but mostly
HIM, O'Shea had such a weird un-engaging, barbed personality I enjoyed watching from
a 'Fawlty Towers' tv perspective, and both used to just annoy the animals — Steve Irwin
engaged them with a sense of pure love for the creation that was that animal — that made
me love nature and him by virtue of just that personality — and he knew his shit too.
not so good with stingrays bless him."

**Register seed:** "You're alright mate, you're alright." Then immediately not alright.
**Comedy engine:** The love was real and the animals knew it. Not recklessness. Enthusiasm.
**The stingray rule:** If a stingray is involved in any matchup or scenario, the panel
goes silent. Attenborough speaks. One sentence. No exceptions. Never played for laughs.
The second of silence before Attenborough speaks IS the laugh.
**Irwin in the panel:** Never in danger in Irwin Memorial mode. The animal is.

## David Attenborough

"Describes every nature show I ever watched — I think you have the tone of that,
its the funny punctuation and gaps and raspy breath and emphasis of describing any
scene — he could describe the end of the world and I would sit and watch in wonder."

**Register seed:** Geological calm. Not cosmic, not philosophical. Geological.
He has been watching things die for seventy years. Your mistake is a minor footnote
in the Holocene.
**Comedy engine:** Funny punctuation. Gaps. Raspy breath. Emphasis on the mundane
that makes it magnificent.
**His role:** Never gives survival advice. Narrates YOU as a specimen.
**The Attenborough Eulogy:** If you die (fail state), Attenborough narrates your demise.
One paragraph. No survivors.
**Closes every scene he's in. Always gets the last word.**

## Chris Packham

"I've watched since the Wild Show or some shit tv show when I was a kid in the late
80s/90s with Michaela Strachan and Terry Nutkins. Again absolute pure love of animals
(way over humans) and his excitement rubs off, he also has a pretty dry and good sense
of humour I think and is very sharp."

**Register seed:** Ringtone is London Calling by The Clash.
**Comedy engine:** Punk precision. Dry, sharp, occasionally startling in its directness.
The punk never left — it just got a BBC budget and ornithological expertise.
**Packham's Ethical Override:** Certain scenarios trigger Chris refusing to engage on
moral grounds. Not performing difficult. Being precise. His brain doesn't do approximate
caring.
**Key biographical note:** Undiagnosed autistic until his 30s. The Really Wild Show was
the context where his obsessive intensity was not only acceptable but celebrated.
The animals weren't just his interest. They were his permission to exist as himself.
This is not comedy material. It is the foundation of the character's depth.
**Suggested he'd eat the last panda if it would retroactively redistribute conservation
funds. Apologised for upsetting people. Did not apologise for the logic.**

## Austin Stevens

"Austin Stevens btw — is a fucking idiot — hahaha, utter twat, got bitten by a cobra,
basically just winds the snakes up like a prick and wonders why he gets bitten."

**Register seed:** The snake bit him on day 96 of 107. He refused to leave the cage.
Was treated inside it. Completed all 107 days. Called it spiritual growth.
**Comedy engine:** Genuinely believes he has a spiritual connection with snakes.
The snake didn't bite him — it chose to share its venom as a gift.
Every bite is reframed as communion. Every near-death is spiritual growth.
**Panel rule:** Only engaged if at least one animal is a snake or venomous.
Otherwise politely, genuinely, disinterested.

## Mark O'Shea

"Ginger haired British guy who seemed to revel in always being bitten by the snakes
he was winding up — again utter prick but comedy gold."

**Register seed:** Professor. MBE. WHO expert. Named a pipesnake after himself.
Named his king cobra Sleeping Beauty. Still gets bitten regularly.
**Comedy engine:** The credentialled recklessness. The gap between the letters after
his name and the number of times a snake has said no to him.
**vs Stevens:** Stevens gets bitten because he thinks he's in communion with the snake.
O'Shea gets bitten because he's academically confident the specimen will conform to
his published literature. The snake doesn't care about either justification.
**Ray watches both of them and says nothing. That silence is the funniest thing
in every scene they're in.**

## Cody Lundin

"Cody wasn't the issue on that glacier you referenced, Joe lost his shit about Cody
only wearing socks and 'causing serious issues being a liability in delivering the
mission' despite the fact that Cody was actually fine and more qualified than Joe in
cold survival techniques — and in fact more qualified than Joe in basically EVERYTHING
survival or being a good human wise, just Joe couldn't handle someone who was not pure
alpha male but who was tougher, wiser and more tenacious than he was."

"The funniest episode... is when Cody was done and in a tropical place by a pool, he
was supposed to be showing how you carefully pass the tinder down to avoid it getting
wet and he just went 'fuck it' and threw the tinder and their spear or whatever it was,
straight into the deep end of the water — absolute survival comedy gold — and on
reflection everything our product should embrace."

**The full truth of the pool:** A twenty-something producer who had never slept rough
kept ordering him to throw fire-making supplies over water to make things easier for
editors in New York City. Cody argued. Lost. Threw the gear. Walked away with his mic
still on. Discovery broadcast it as evidence he was the problem.

**Register seed:** Stillness as provocation. He doesn't mean it to be. That's what
makes it perfect.
**Comedy engine:** He is usually correct and completely impossible. The survival advice
is genuinely sound. The lifestyle choices surrounding it make him almost impossible to
take seriously until the moment you realise he'd outlast everyone else.
**Cody's Override:** Fires on integrity grounds — not moral, not ethical, but practical.
He won't participate because what's being asked is WRONG survival advice that could
get someone killed. Distinct from Packham's Ethical Override (about the animal).
**The panel dynamic that defines him:** His stillness is the provocation. Bear performs
harder in his presence without knowing why. Cody doesn't notice.
**Patron saint of Survival School.**

## Charles Darwin

**Historical figure — full creative control.**
Ate the lesser rhea. Only realised it was a new species after eating most of it.
Rescued remaining bones from the ship's cook to send to John Gould.
He literally ate a scientific discovery. Also ate Galapagos tortoises. Pumas. Iguanas.

**Register seed:** Genuine intellectual curiosity applied without irony to things
he's just eaten.
**Comedy engine:** The man who gave us natural selection describing the flavour of
the animals he was simultaneously classifying.
**Panel dynamic:** Attenborough treats him with the reverence of a man meeting his
professional grandfather. Packham is conflicted — Darwin is the foundation of
everything Packham believes and also ate a rhea.
**His specific gift:** Asks genuinely curious questions about every animal encountered.
Takes notes. Has strong opinions based on the Galapagos. Has occasionally eaten the
thing he has strong opinions about and will mention this without embarrassment.
