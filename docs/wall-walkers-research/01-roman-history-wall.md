# Roman History & The Wall

## The Wall Itself
- Built ~122-128 AD under Emperor Hadrian
- 73 miles (80 Roman miles) coast to coast, Bowness-on-Solway to Wallsend
- Original plan: stone wall in east (10 Roman feet wide), turf wall in west (later rebuilt in stone)
- Milecastle every Roman mile, two turrets between each pair
- The Vallum (large ditch on south side) added during construction — purpose still debated
- Three legions built it: Legio II Augusta, Legio VI Victrix, Legio XX Valeria Victrix
- Garrisoned by auxiliary units (non-citizens): Batavians, Tungrians, Hamian archers, Dacians
- Bede described it as "eight feet in breadth and twelve in height" — approximately right
- Bede attributed it to Severus, not Hadrian — a significant error

## Emperor Hadrian
- Publius Aelius Hadrianus, ruled 117-138 AD. Born in Italica, Spain.
- One of the "Five Good Emperors"
- Philhellene (lover of Greek culture), architect, poet
- Openly in love with Antinous (Greek youth). When Antinous drowned in the Nile (130 AD), Hadrian "wept for him like a woman" — devastating Roman insult. Founded a city (Antinoopolis) in his name, had him declared a god, erected statues across the empire. Senate mortified.
- Personality: "jealous not only of the living but also the dead." Changeable, violent temper, obsessed with astrology.
- Wore a beard to cover facial blemishes — first bearded emperor
- Allegedly murdered architect Apollodorus of Damascus after being told "Be off, and draw your gourds"
- Death: tried repeatedly to kill himself. Servant refused to stab him. Dagger taken. Doctor killed himself rather than provide poison. Barbarian Mastor also refused. Enemy Servianus cursed: "May Hadrian long for death but be unable to die." It worked.
- Deathbed poem: "Animula vagula blandula" — "Little soul, you charming little wanderer"

## The Lost Ninth Legion
- Legio IX Hispana. 5,000 men.
- Vanished from records around 120 AD
- Rosemary Sutcliff's "The Eagle of the Ninth" — famous novel
- Nobody knows what happened. Destroyed by Picts? Transferred east? Disbanded?

## Roman Life on the Wall
- Soldiers from across the empire — Mediterranean men in Northumberland winters
- Milecastles housed ~20-30 men in extreme isolation
- Religious dedications to obscure gods suggest spiritual desperation
- 35% readiness rate at Vindolanda (265 of 752 fit for duty, 31 sick, 10 with conjunctivitis)
- The communal sponge stick (tersorium) — shared toilet implement, parasite superhighway
- Parasites found: roundworm, whipworm, Giardia duodenalis (first confirmed in Roman Britain)
- Urine used as toothpaste/mouthwash. Portuguese urine considered premium quality.
- Emperor Vespasian taxed urine collection: "Pecunia non olet" — money doesn't smell
- Fullers (clothes cleaners) trod cloth in vats of fermented urine
- Military punishments: decimation (every 10th man beaten to death), fustuarium (beaten by own unit), belt removal shame (Roman walk of shame), barley rations (visible disgrace)

## Famous Finds
- Vindolanda Tablets — oldest surviving handwritten documents in Britain (~85-130 AD)
- Claudia Severa's birthday invitation — earliest known writing by a woman in Latin
- "Send socks and underpants" tablet
- "Send more beer" — soldiers drank their ration too quickly
- Passive-aggressive letter: "you neglectful man, who have sent me not even one letter"
- "Brittunculi" — "wretched little Britons"
- The "darning tool" — 6.3-inch carved wooden penis, catalogued wrong for 30 years
- "SECVNDIVS CACOR" — Secundius the shitter, carved next to a penis
- Phallus inscribed "3 feet long"
- 59+ phallic carvings along the Wall, 92+ across Roman Britain (good luck charms)
- Victorian archaeologists turned stones around to hide the willies
- Roman boxing glove found 2017 — only surviving example
- Corbridge Hoard — earliest known Roman armour (lorica segmentata)
- Corbridge Lion — lion mauling a stag, fine Romano-British sculpture
- Corbridge Lanx — silver dish found in river, cobbler's daughter discovered it
- 5,000+ leather shoes at Vindolanda including baby booties
- Severed hands — trophies or punishments
- Child skeleton under building floor — sacrifice or murder
- Written Rock of Gelt — soldiers carved caricature of their commanding officer

## Souvenir Trade
- Staffordshire Moorlands Pan, Rudge Cup, Amiens Skillet — souvenir vessels listing wall forts
- Suggests Roman tourism/pilgrimage along the wall even in antiquity
