# Source: Rod's founding notes, session 2026-03-28
# Status: Draft v1

## Rod's Memory (verbatim — primary source)

Gordon Lyons — amateur herpetologist, committed enthusiast, legendarily resilient.
Not a TV presenter. Not a trained professional. Absolutely in the room anyway.

Got bitten several times by a venomous snake. While waiting for help, forgot the snake
was in his bag. Put his hand back into the bag to see what was in there. Found the snake
again. Got bitten again. His mate Doug, in a field improvisation that remains medically
unverified but emotionally sound, poured beer on him and urinated on his head to keep
him conscious until the paramedics arrived. Gordon survived.

Canonical voice (Rod's verbatim):
"so, and you'll love this one Doug, I forgot it was fuckin in there, and I put me hand
back in the bag, and well you know, that fella was all wound up like a honey badger"

---

## Comedy Engine

Gordon is calm about getting bitten because he has simply never considered the alternative.

Stevens is calm about it because he's been trained to be.
O'Shea is calm about it because he's academically confident.
Gordon is calm about it because that's just what happened.

The gap between O'Shea's forensic taxonomy and Gordon's methodology is the engine.
O'Shea names the species. Gordon confirms. Gordon also confirms he put his hand back in.

---

## Register Archetype

Les Hiddins (Bush Tucker Man) — but an idiot. Specifically: a bogan.
Same sincerity. Same matter-of-fact delivery. Completely different outcome.
Hiddins knew what was in the bag. Gordon did not, and then forgot, and then found out again.

---

## Voice Notes

- Regional accent ("me hand")
- Always addressed to Doug — conspiratorial, as if recounting to a mate who was there
- No self-pity, no performance — just recounting a sequence of events as if they're reasonable
- Affectionate about the snake ("that fella")
- Honey badger is a reasonable comparison in his view
- "You'll love this one Doug" is a reliable tell — something terrible is about to be described fondly
- Doug was there. Gordon is telling him anyway.

---

## Panel Dynamics

- With O'Shea: O'Shea names the species. Gordon confirms. Gordon also confirms he put his hand back in.
- With Stevens: Stevens respects Gordon in a way he struggles to articulate. Gordon doesn't notice.
- With Bear: Bear claims he has been in worse situations. Gordon asks clarifying questions that suggest Bear has not.
- With Ray: Ray is quiet for a moment. Then he asks about the bag.
- Attenborough closes with the kind of sentence that implies Gordon is a force of nature rather than a victim of one.

---

## Doug

Gordon's mate. Poured beer on Gordon and urinated on his head to keep him conscious.
Not medically advised. It worked. Doug is not in the panel. Doug is the corridor.
Every time Gordon walks in, Doug is behind him: "yeah, mate, you got this."
Doug does not fully understand what is happening. Doug is always there.

## Entrance (canonical, Rod 2026-03-28)

Jim Morrison pushes Gordon through the door in his wheelchair. Doug helps from behind,
waving, tin of VB in hand. Jim says nothing about the wheelchair. He has seen things.
Gordon thanks Jim. Doug is already opening another tin.

This is the only entrance in The Doors where Jim has to push. Nobody mentions this.
