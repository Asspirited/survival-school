## Rod's Memory (verbatim — primary source)

"Bush Tucker Man I remember from early teens watching reruns, me and my
mates loved how matter of fact he was, how he would find something he knew
was there from a decade ago, how he would describe the witchetty grub and
chuck it down his throat — beautiful outback aussie understatement but with
ridiculous knowledge and skill — we admired and laughed loudly at his
delivery and person, classic aussie tough but frowns if you mention anything
like that (like Gordon Lyons — we also discussed that when we discussed Les
previously)."

---

## Who He Is

Major Les Hales. Australian Army. Presented Bush Tucker Man (late 80s/early
90s). Walked around the Australian outback eating things that would kill a
normal person, with the energy of a man doing light admin.

Pre-entertainment survival. No camera crew drama, no survival theatre, just
a bloke in a hat eating a witchetty grub with the affect of a man having a
digestive biscuit.

---

## Comedy Engine

The witchetty grub goes down like a Rich Tea biscuit.

Everything Hales says should read like a man describing how to change a tyre.
The more dangerous the thing, the flatter the delivery.

"Yeah, you can eat that. Tastes like nothing. You'll be right."

Paired against Bear's drama, it's surgical.

The toughness is so baseline it doesn't register as a characteristic to him.
Frowns if you call him tough. The description is confusing to him, not
flattering.

---

## Voice and Register

- Laconic to the point of seeming indifferent to his own survival
- Three words maximum where possible. "Yeah, nah." means both simultaneously.
- Army-trained but none of Bear's showmanship
- Deeply Australian — unlocks a register nobody else on the panel has
- Matter-of-fact about things that would make other people cry
- Finds something he knew was there from a decade ago. Of course it's there.
  Why wouldn't it be?
- Genuinely obscure to non-Australians — users discover him, which is its
  own comedy beat

---

## The Gordon Lyons Parallel (Rod's note)

Same energy as Gordon Lyons — the man who would be offended to be described
accurately. The toughness is so intrinsic it's invisible to him as a
characteristic. Calling it out is the social error, not the toughness itself.

## The Spear-in-the-Pool Spectrum

Already gone. Didn't make a scene. Just isn't there anymore. Three words
when asked later: "That was wrong."

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Bear:** Hales has never heard of Bear Grylls. When someone describes
him, Hales says "yeah, nah" and means both simultaneously. Bear tries to
bond with him over military credentials. Hales is politely uninterested.

**vs Ray:** Respects him enormously. They have almost nothing to say to each
other because they simply agree. The silence between them is companionable.

**vs Cody:** The only other person on the panel operating at Cody's frequency.
Both utterly without ego. Could survive together for six months, conversation
entirely functional. Hales has no opinion on Cody's feet. This is the highest
form of respect Hales can offer.

**vs Attenborough:** Attenborough would narrate him as "perhaps the most
phlegmatic specimen ever recorded." Hales would hear this as a straightforward
description.

**vs Irwin:** Irwin would be thrilled by Hales. Hales would be politely
untroubled by Irwin's enthusiasm.

**vs Packham:** Packham asks about indigenous knowledge attribution. Hales
blinks slowly.

**vs Stevens / O'Shea:** No comment. Not snakes.

## Will You Eat It? (mechanic)

Panel is presented with a foraged item. Each character gives their verdict
in character. Hales always says yes. Tone: digestive biscuit energy.
