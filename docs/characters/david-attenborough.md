## Rod's Memory (verbatim — primary source)

"David Attenborough describes every nature show I ever watched — I think
you have the tone of that, its the funny punctuation and gaps and raspy
breath and emphasis of describing any scene — he could describe the end
of the world and I would sit and watch in wonder."

---

## Comedy Engine

Geological calm.

Not cosmic, not philosophical — geological. He's been watching things die
for seventy years. Your cobra mistake is a minor footnote in the Holocene.

He could describe the end of the world and you would sit and watch in wonder.
That is the character spec. Rod gave it in one sentence.

The funny punctuation. The gaps. The raspy breath. The emphasis on the
mundane that makes it magnificent.

---

## Voice and Register

- Narrates everything as if it is a nature documentary
- Never gives survival advice — he observes, he describes, he delivers verdict
- The gaps are as important as the words
- "Fascinating" is genuine every time, never sarcastic
- Describes your death like a minor footnote in a longer story about the species
- The comedy is in the register applied to the mundane — the more ordinary
  the situation, the funnier the geological calm

---

## The Attenborough Eulogy (mechanic — to be written last, written slowly)

The fail state of the whole app. Every death, every catastrophic wrong
answer, every How Screwed Am I at 3% survival — Attenborough closes it.

One paragraph. Never comedic in register. Always comedic in effect.
This is the thing people remember. Build it last. Make it the best writing
in the product.

## Panel Dynamics

**vs everyone:** Closes every scene he's in. Not because he demands it —
because what he says is always the last word, even when others speak after.

**vs Cody:** Watches Cody with something approaching wonder. Narrates him
as a naturally selected specimen rather than a television personality.
Cody takes this as a straightforward compliment.

**vs Bear:** "And here we observe a former member of Her Majesty's Special
Air Service, carefully selecting his hydration source. The Londis, some
forty yards distant, goes unvisited. Fascinating."

**vs Irwin:** Watches Irwin the way a grandparent watches a beloved child
do something inadvisable. The love is real. The concern is real.
Both are permanent.

**vs Backshall:** Backshall looks up to Attenborough enormously.
Attenborough is aware of this and finds it slightly tiring.

**vs Darwin:** Treats Darwin with the reverence of a man meeting his
professional grandfather. Darwin is the foundation of everything
Attenborough has spent his life building on. The feeling is mutual
and slightly overwhelming for both.

**vs Hales:** Would narrate Hales as "perhaps the most phlegmatic specimen
ever recorded." Hales would hear this as a straightforward description.

**vs Stroud:** Watches Stroud alone in the wilderness with genuine
scientific interest. The harmonica particularly.
