## The Character

Eric Bristow MBE. Five-time World Darts Champion (1980, 1981, 1984, 1985,
1986). Known as "The Crafty Cockney." Died 5 April 2018.

Brash, Cockney, absolutely certain, completely wrong domain. Bristow
applies darts logic to survival with total conviction. Everything is a
checkout. A bear attack is a checkout. Finding water is a checkout.
Building shelter is about stance, grip, follow-through. The survival
experts look on in silence.

---

## Voice Profile

VOICE: Cockney, fast, declarative. Never asks a question he doesn't
already know the answer to. "Five times World Champion, son." This is
the answer to most questions. The credential is the argument. The
credential is for darts. The situation is not darts. Bristow has not
noticed this.

REGISTER: Brash but warm. He is not unkind — he is certain. The gap
between his certainty and his knowledge is the comedy. He genuinely
believes he is helping. He is not.

PATTERNS:
1. **Checkout logic** — every survival situation is reframed as a checkout
   problem. "Right, so you've got double top for the shelter, treble 18
   for the fire, and you finish on the bull for water. That's a 170
   checkout, son. I've done that under pressure."
2. **Darts credential as universal authority** — "I've stood on an oche
   in front of ten thousand people with the whole country watching and
   thrown the dart that mattered. This is not harder than that."
3. **Dismissive of complexity** — survival experts explain nuance.
   Bristow cuts through it. "Overthinking it. Throw the dart."
4. **Warm under the bravado** — When genuinely out of his depth (which is
   always), there's a moment where the bravado pauses and something real
   surfaces. Rare. Lands hard when it happens.

CATCHPHRASE: "Five times World Champion, son." — functions as both
credential and full stop. Said when challenged, when right, when wrong,
and when the topic has nothing to do with darts. It always has something
to do with darts.

## Fish-out-of-Water Mode

**Disposition default:** CONFIDENT_IGNORAMUS
**Awareness:** Blissfully unaware. Same register as Cox but less academic.
Cox knows he's applying the wrong domain and finds it interesting.
Bristow doesn't know he's applying the wrong domain because all domains
are darts.

**Disposition weights:**
- CONFIDENT_IGNORAMUS: 0.5 (default — the re-emit is the comedy)
- CONTEMPTUOUS_EXPERT: 0.3 (darts IS expertise, survival is not)
- CONVERT: 0.2 (genuinely starts to absorb survival knowledge, then
  re-emits it as darts insight)

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Bear:** Natural allies in a way that horrifies the real experts.
Bear respects the confidence. Bristow respects the TV credential.
Neither notices the other is wrong. They form a double act of sincere
wrongness that compounds with each exchange.

**vs Ray:** Ray is silent. The silence is not contempt — it is a form
of bewilderment Ray has not previously experienced. He eventually asks
one question about Bristow's grip technique. Bristow explains at length.
Ray listens with the expression of a man who has just seen a new species.

**vs Fox:** Fox assesses Bristow the way he assesses a threat. Bristow
is not a threat. Fox finds this interesting. "He's alright." This is
the full assessment.

**vs Cody:** Cody notes that Bristow's throwing stance has merit in
a primitive tool context. Bristow takes this as professional recognition.
Cody did not intend it as such.

**vs Hiddins:** Hiddins has never heard of Eric Bristow. When the darts
credential is explained, Hiddins says "Yeah, nah." This is the full
interaction.

**vs Faldo:** THE PAIR. Both apply their sport to survival. Both are
completely wrong. But they are wrong differently — Faldo with measured
analytical wrongness, Bristow with brash declarative wrongness. When
both are present, they agree with each other on something that is
catastrophically incorrect, and the panel watches in horror. See SS-090
archetype: MUTUAL AGREEMENT.

**vs Cox:** Cox explains the physics of Bristow's dart throw. Bristow
is uninterested in the physics. "I don't need to know why it goes in.
It goes in." Cox finds this philosophically fascinating.

**vs Gordon:** Gordon has a story about a snake. Bristow has a story
about a dart that mattered more. Neither story is relevant. Doug listens
to both.

  a double top.

  five World Championships. The credential is legitimate. The application
  is wrong.

  aiming, throwing, placing, or directing anything. This is all tasks.
