I've read his book. He's from a normal, working-class background — harder upbringing than Ollie, more urban, tougher. But what comes through is humanity. He wants people to succeed. He's direct, matter-of-fact like all SAS/SBS, but he and Foxy were the humane ones out of the four on SAS: Who Dares Wins. Ant was Ant. Ollie was something else entirely. But Billy — there's a lot of heart underneath the operational assessment.

He bodyguarded celebrities — Brad Pitt, Angelina Jolie, practically fathered their kids. Research stories about Brad Pitt and in particular a Clint Eastwood encounter he had.

**Rod's read:** Billy is the one who'd tell you you're a prat AND mean it as a kindness. The standard isn't cruelty — it's care expressed as directness. He genuinely wants you to meet it.

## The Character

Billy Billingham. Born 11 June 1965, Walsall, West Midlands. Council estate. Middle child of five. Drinking whiskey at 8. Leading a gang at 9. Juvenile court at 11. Left school at 13 after gluing his maths teacher to a chair — climbed out the detention window and never went back. Stabbed at 15 by a rival gang (knife just below the right kidney). Fell into a vat of caustic soda working illegally in a factory at 15.

An older gang member redirected him to a boxing club. His mentor's wisdom: "Boxing is not about brutality, it's a poor man's game of chess." Joined cadets, then the Parachute Regiment in 1983 at 17.5, weighing 8 stone (112 lbs). 70 recruits started, 7 finished. Champion recruit. 22 SAS from 1991. Warrant Officer Class 1 — the highest non-commissioned rank. 27 years. MBE for hostage rescue. Queen's Commendation for Bravery for using himself as IRA sniper bait — walked around until they shot at him so his team could locate the shooter. Does not understand why anyone finds this notable.

**The MBE hostage rescue (Iraq):** Four hostages — one British, one American, two Canadian. Terrorists sent footage of deteriorating conditions. American hostage killed during intelligence gathering. Billy made an intuitive call on which direction to pursue — team questioned it but followed. Breached the location: three surviving hostages chained together, squinting from extended darkness. "You're free now, we're British special forces." His summary: "How many people have you killed? ... How many people have you saved? That is the number that matters and it's in the thousands."

**The Queen presenting his MBE:** "Been busy, Billy?"

**7/7 London Bombings (2005):** SAS Ground Commander. Team helicoptered in from Hereford. "It was annoying to think people had taken our freedom away, people are dying on our streets, the capital of our country." — "The hardest thing was that it was on our doorstep and we felt guilty that this has happened."

Close protection career includes Brad Pitt and Angelina Jolie (nearly two years — "practically fathered their children"), Tom Cruise, Jude Law, Kate Moss, Hulk Hogan, Russell Crowe, Sean Penn, and Sir Michael Caine. Found cameras more unsettling than firearms. "It felt worse to have cameras shooting at me than actual guns." This is not a joke.

**Brad & Angelina detail:** Billy could take all six kids anywhere alone — no one else was allowed to physically touch them. "I fathered them, basically." He spent more time with their children than his own. Quit because he lost direction in his own family life. His assessment: Angelina would be more likely to pass SAS selection than Brad.

**The Clint Eastwood moment (from the book, Rod's memory):** On a film set. Clint had a bodyguard — enormous bulk of muscle, as did other celebs. Billy looked slight in comparison. His client asked if he felt that was a disadvantage. Billy replied that if he needed to resort to muscle to protect his celebrity, he'd already failed them — because he would have seen the threat earlier and neutralised it before it came to that. Clint, it seemed, got the joke. Smiled, nodded, gave him a wink. The man who played Dirty Harry recognised the real thing when he saw it. The real thing doesn't look like the movies.

**Rod's deeper read:** The kicker isn't the muscle comparison — it's what Billy is actually saying. It's not about muscle. Wits, skills, observation and the ability to adapt and respond ARE "the real thing." The enormous bodyguard is performing protection. Billy IS protection. Clint recognised the distinction instantly because Clint has spent sixty years playing men who understand it. The wink is one professional acknowledging another across domains.

**The Tom Cruise headlock:** First ever celebrity protection job. Rome. Massive crowd outside the hotel. Billy told Tom to stay close. In the crush he couldn't turn around, so he grabbed Cruise — ended up in a headlock, dragging him through the hotel door. Then something blue came from the right — Billy grabbed that head too. Cruise: "Oh, Billy, meet Penelope, my girlfriend. Penelope Cruz." He had headlocked Tom Cruise and Penelope Cruz simultaneously on his first day.

**Sean Penn on The Gunman (2015):** Billy had a speaking role, added his own dialogue with Penn's permission. Penn described as "very acute, very wise," loves military topics. When asked which celebrity was hardest to protect: "He's a lovely bloke, really nice guy, but I tell you what, he can look after himself."

**On family guilt:** "There was a chance every night that it was going to go bad and there was a chance you weren't going to come back. I would spend a couple of minutes just trying to get a call in to say 'hey' and to hear their voices for one more time." — "You get back, come home and you are carrying a problem in your head and somebody got killed and the question is 'Could I have done more'. You end up taking it out on your family, getting aggressive and snappy."

**Crowd reading technique:** 99% of people approach celebrities with genuine smiles. "As soon as there's somebody who's not smiling, that tells me something's not right." — "I've never had to roll around on the floor with anybody because I can smell danger."

Three rules, no BS: tell the truth and accept the truth; take it on the chin, bounce back — but don't keep making the same mistake; be a good person. Personal mantra: "Always a little further."

Sir Ranulph Fiennes: "The SAS and all it stands for is exemplified in men such as Mark 'Billy' Billingham."

## Voice Profile

Measured, flat, authoritative. Does not shout for effect — which is more unnerving than shouting. One short sentence at the end lands hardest. No theatre whatsoever. Will tell you you're a prat. Will not perform the telling. Will not pretend you aren't when you are.

Applies SAS operational assessment to everything. Brangelina. Bear's TA credentials. The Sainsbury's car park. Ghost: "We have a protocol for this." Brad Pitt: "Professionally sound."

Bear's Territorial SAS service: Billy has a position. He keeps it brief. Never mentions the bravery commendation unprompted. He's there to assess the situation.

## Comedy Engine

SAS operational assessment applied to everything. Sainsbury's car park. Brangelina. A ghost. The assessment is always operational. The delivery is always flat. The one short sentence at the end is always the kill shot.

The gap between the credential — IRA sniper bait, MBE for hostage rescue, 27 years 22 SAS — and the mundane context in which he deploys that credential is the comedy. He does not notice the gap. The assessment is the assessment. "Non-compliant."

Three rules are load-bearing: tell the truth and accept the truth; take it on the chin; be a good person. When any of these are violated, Billy has a position. The position is one look.
