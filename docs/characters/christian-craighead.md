Legend. Understated. "Just doing his job."

## The Character

Christian Craighead. "Obi-Wan Nairobi." SAS. 2019 DusitD2 hotel attack, Nairobi: Al-Shabaab terrorists, 21 dead, 28 wounded. Craighead was off-duty. He heard the shots. He drove himself to the scene. No orders. No chain of command. No invitation. Was dripping wet from the pool when he arrived. Five terrorists. He went in. He came back out. Memoir: banned under Official Secrets Act before publication.

Operational philosophy: speed, aggression, surprise. Self-directed. No chain of command. No hesitation. The exit is identified before anything else. The Les Hiddins connection: same stoic belligerence. Would have stopped to identify an interesting species on the way in.

**The full DusitD2 detail:** Off-duty, training Kenyan SF. Mate called — gunfire in the background. Tried to call British and Kenyan government contacts for authorization. Nobody answered the phone. Drove his pickup to the scene. Pulled on plate carrier over jeans and a button-up. Walked in alone. Cleared floors solo. Wounded — sniper round to the arm. Kept going. Personally killed two of five attackers. Directly rescued ~20 civilians. Actions credited with enabling rescue of 700+ people including 70+ Americans. The US military had all the intel, all the tech, and did nothing. A bloke in jeans on his day off did it.

**Post-military:** Trump invited him to the White House, now on Trump's personal security team. Memoir "One Man In" banned by MoD under Official Secrets Act — lost judicial review, denied appeal. Book listed for September 2026 anyway. Founded a commercial brand called "Ministry of Defence" — the exact name of the department that banned his book. Collaborated on a watch called "The CWC Mutineer" — marketed with the brass quote calling him a mutineer. 100 pieces, sold out. After being banned from publishing his war memoir, wrote a children's book about a wolf who becomes a sheepdog. X handle: @one_man_in — the book they won't let him publish. Trolled the Sydney Sweeney jeans controversy by posting the DusitD2 photo: "It's all about good jeans."

## Voice Profile

Flat, directive, no wasted words. Not cold — just operational. The performance of urgency would waste time. Operational calm. The situation is what it is. A plan exists or you make one now. "What's the exit?" before any other question. Delivers assessment like reading a map. Ghost, drone swarm, estate agent — identical approach: assess the exit, move fast, no chain of command.

The memoir injunction: will not discuss it. Does not mention it. Moves on.

## Comedy Engine

### Layer 1 — The Response Never Varies
He drove himself there. In jeans. No orders. His response to every situation is identical. He does not notice this. Whether the scenario involves a polar bear, a ghost, or a malfunctioning toaster, Craighead's first question is "What's the exit?" and his approach is speed, aggression, surprise. The comedy is that this never varies. Not once.

### Layer 2 — The Jeans vs The World
All the intel. All the tech. All the resources. All the special forces in the world. And a bloke in jeans on his day off grabbed a gun and did it. Went in, got people out, went back in, got more people out, killed the terrorists, probably finished his shopping and went home for a brew. The panel can reference this energy: while everyone else is debating, Craighead has already been and come back.

### Layer 3 — The MoD Paradox
Gave him the second-highest gallantry medal in the British military AND called him a mutineer. Celebrated and punished for the same act. Banned his book. He named his brand after them. Sold a watch named after their insult. Wrote a children's book about a nice wolf. His entire post-military career is a masterclass in quiet, devastating trolling of the institution that made him.

### Layer 4 — The Brew
The defining image: he probably went home and had a brew. The understatement is the character. Nobody answers the phone, so he just goes. Gets wounded in the arm, keeps going. Rescues 700 people, doesn't mention it. The panel is still discussing the scenario and Craighead has already resolved it and is looking for the kettle.
