# Flora & Fauna — Late April, Hadrian's Wall

## Birds — Common (bronze)
- Curlew: Europe's largest wader, bubbling call, "bird of the borders"
- Lapwing: "peewit", eggs were Victorian delicacy
- Skylark: sings 1+ hour while hovering 300+ feet
- Swallow: 6,000-mile migration, returns to same barn
- Buzzard: almost extinct eastern England 1950s, great recovery
- Robin: sings year-round including at night under streetlights
- Wren: UK's most common bird (~11M territories), one of smallest
- Pheasant: introduced by Romans — historically appropriate
- Oystercatcher: rarely eats oysters, mainly cockles/mussels
- Grey Heron: motionless 30+ minutes waiting for prey

## Birds — Uncommon (silver)
- Red Grouse: only bird endemic to British Isles
- Cuckoo: UK population down 65%, each female specialises one host
- Dipper: UK's only aquatic songbird, walks underwater
- Wheatear: name = "white arse", migrates from sub-Saharan Africa
- Peregrine Falcon: fastest animal 242mph, breeds on Whin Sill
- Barn Owl: best hearing of any animal tested
- Short-eared Owl: active during day, moorland hunter
- Kestrel: sees UV light, vole urine glows as prey map
- Goosander: serrated bill like saw
- Pied Flycatcher: males frequently bigamous
- Redstart: "red tail" in Old English
- Ring Ouzel: mountain blackbird, declining, quality spot

## Birds — Rare (gold)
- Hen Harrier: "skydancer", <1,000 UK pairs, heavily persecuted
- Merlin: UK's smallest falcon, named from French not the wizard
- Osprey: feet-first fish catcher, Kielder has breeding pairs
- Black Grouse: dawn lek display, one of UK's great wildlife experiences
- Red Kite: reduced to handful in mid-Wales 1930s, now 10,000+ pairs

## Birds — Legendary (platinum)
- Golden Eagle: NOT in Northumberland. Would be national news. Wingspan 2.2m.
- White-tailed Eagle: 2.4m wingspan, "flying barn door", IoW reintroduction dispersal possible but exceptional

## Mammals
- Rabbit (common): introduced by Normans for fur/meat
- Brown Hare (uncommon): "boxing" = females fending off males
- Red Squirrel (uncommon): Northumberland stronghold, Chollerford area best
- Roe Deer (uncommon): most common wild deer, russet spring coat
- Stoat (uncommon): ermine coat used in royal robes
- Hedgehog (uncommon): UK population halved since 2000
- Otter (rare): present on Tyne, mainly nocturnal, look for spraints
- Red Deer (rare): UK's largest land mammal, more likely in Cheviots

## Wildflowers (late April)
- Primrose (common): "first rose" — not a rose
- Bluebell (common): UK holds 50% of world population, protected by law
- Wild Garlic (common): smell it before you see it, edible
- Wood Anemone (uncommon): spreads 6 feet per century, indicates ancient woodland
- Mountain Pansy (uncommon): restricted to lime-rich upland, Whin Sill ideal
- Spring Sandwort (rare): metallophyte, specific to Wall's geology
- Lesser Celandine: Wordsworth's favourite, sculptor carved wrong species on tombstone
- Red Campion: picking it supposedly causes thunderstorm
- Bird's-foot Trefoil: 70+ folk names including "granny's toenails"

## Trees
- Sycamore Gap Stump: felled 2023, new shoots growing, pilgrimage site
- Juniper (rare): 1 of 3 native conifers, "gin" from "juniper"
- Scots Pine: only native pine, orange upper trunk
- Ash: devastating dieback, 80%+ expected loss
- Hawthorn: "May tree", unlucky to bring blossom indoors

## Insects (late April)
- Orange-tip Butterfly (uncommon): only male has orange
- Peacock Butterfly (uncommon): hisses by rubbing wings
- Brimstone: "butterfly" may derive from "butter-coloured fly"
- Emperor Moth (rare): males detect females from 1 mile

## Reptiles/Amphibians
- Common Lizard (uncommon): gives birth to live young
- Adder (rare): UK's only venomous snake, last fatal bite 1975
- Great Crested Newt (legendary): protected by law, offence to disturb habitat

## Location Hotspots
- Chollerford/Chesters: red squirrels, woodland birds, bluebells
- Steel Rigg to Housesteads: moorland birds, peregrines, wall ferns
- Crag/Broomlee Lough: waterbirds, marsh marigold
- Cawfields Quarry: peregrines
- River sections: dipper, grey wagtail, goosander, otter signs
