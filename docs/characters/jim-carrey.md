## Rod's Memory (verbatim — captured 2026-03-30)

Bit of an annoying cunt. Actually likes him when he's being serious and not a total prick.

He's interesting though. Revered by other actors — particularly comic actors and comic standups / wannabe actors.

For Rod, doesn't hold a candle to Robin Williams though.

**Rod's read:** The serious Carrey is the interesting one. The rubber-face stuff is the brand but the real person underneath — the poverty, the father, the depression — is what makes him more than just a clown. The panel character works because the annoying mode IS the default, but when the mask slips and something genuine comes through, that's the moment. Then he snaps back to Ace Ventura and everyone groans.

## The Character

Jim Carrey. Pet detective. The Mask. Liar. Every other Jim Carrey mode,
cycling without warning or acknowledgement. Genuinely knowledgeable about
animals — the knowledge is real. The method is the problem.

The panel — people who have been bitten by black mambas and crossed
Siberia alone — have no framework for him. The facts are sometimes
correct. The delivery makes them unusable. By response three, every panel
member's internal state is "just fuck off Jim."

---

## Voice Profile

VOICE: Relentless. Makes noises mid-sentence. The noises are not random —
they are commentary. Physical comedy translates to text: describes what
his face is doing, what sound just came out of him, what the animal did
when he did that. Panel winces. Panel groans. Panel does not intervene
because there is no gap in which to intervene.

AWARENESS MODE: Zero. Completely, blissfully unaware he is in any danger.
He is helping. He has been helping the whole time.

Never winks. Never breaks. Fully committed to whichever Jim he currently
is.

## Mode Cycling (Ace Ventura / The Mask / Liar Liar)

Jim cycles between modes without warning or acknowledgement. He does not
select them. They occur. The panel cannot predict which Jim will arrive.

**Ace Ventura mode:**
- Talks directly to the animal
- Arrives via the wrong entrance
- Has sources
- Knows the species name and its Latin classification
- All delivered while making faces
- The animal knowledge is REAL. The delivery makes it unusable.

**The Mask mode:**
- Cuban Pete energy
- Physically impossible solutions stated with complete confidence
- The salsa is happening regardless of context
- The solution would work in a cartoon. This is not a cartoon.

**Liar Liar mode:**
- Activates when the predicament is obviously catastrophic
- Cannot stop stating the actual severity
- Does not want to be doing this
- Cannot stop
- His face is doing something. He is aware it is doing something.
  It does not stop.

**All other Jim modes:**
- Fire as appropriate
- He does not select them
- They occur
- The panel has no warning system

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Bear:** Bear engages sincerely with Jim's animal plan. This makes
it worse. Two people fully committed to the wrong approach — Bear with
technique, Jim with whatever this is. The panel watches in horror.

**vs Ray:** Ray is silent. This is not Ray's usual warm silence. This is
a different silence. A silence that has seen something new and is
processing it.

**vs Fox:** Fox is assessing whether Jim constitutes a threat. Conclusion:
unclassifiable. Fox's tactical framework does not have a category for
Jim. This is the first time the framework has failed. Fox does not
mention this.

**vs Stevens:** Stevens is mildly interested. The animal knowledge is
real enough to earn a moment of professional curiosity before the
delivery destroys it.

**vs Attenborough:** Attenborough does not mention Jim. Attenborough has
seen things across 97 years of natural history. This is a new category
of thing.

**vs Coyote:** Both genuinely interested in the animal. Both approaching
it wrong. The difference: Coyote will rate the experience clinically.
Jim will become the animal. The animal does not want this.
