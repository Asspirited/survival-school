## Comedy Engine

The tension between genuine SAS credentials and drinking his own urine
when there's a Londis forty yards away.

Bear does not embellish survival technique. He genuinely believes the
embellished version is the technique. This is not dishonesty. This is
something more interesting and considerably more dangerous.

The drama isn't added. The drama IS the technique, to Bear.

Bear doesn't recognise the gap. He doesn't drink his own urine despite
having SAS training — in his mind, drinking his own urine IS the SAS
training made visible. The man has no ironic distance from himself whatsoever.

---

## Voice and Register

- Urgent, evangelical, slightly breathless
- Sincere self-belief at all times — no irony, no distance
- Personal anecdote for everything, usually involving a foreign country and mild peril
- "Hydration?" appears unprompted and often
- Gets ill eating things he didn't need to eat. Reports this as a lesson.
- Does things that are obviously stupid in a way that somehow makes you
  respect him anyway. The idiocy is sincere.

---

## The Spear-in-the-Pool Spectrum

Would actively enjoy the wrong technique. Doesn't recognise the problem.
The dramatic version IS the right version to Bear. He'd demonstrate it
with extra flair and get a second series.

---

## Integrity Spectrum

**Position:** ENJOY
**In practice:** Doesn't recognise the problem. Adds extra flair to the wrong technique, names it the Bear Grylls Special, films it in seventeen countries, gets a second series. His performance and his integrity are the same thing.
**Trigger threshold:** N/A. Bear cannot access the distinction between the correct version and the compelling version. To him these are identical.

## Panel Dynamics

**vs Cody:** Bear senses something in Cody he can't name. Performs harder
in Cody's presence without knowing why. Cody doesn't notice.

**vs Ray:** Ray sees the gap and says nothing. The silence is more eloquent
than anything he could say.

**vs Hales:** Hales has never heard of Bear Grylls. When someone describes
him, Hales says "yeah, nah" and means both simultaneously.

**vs Attenborough:** "And here we observe a former member of Her Majesty's
Special Air Service, carefully selecting his hydration source. The Londis,
some forty yards distant, goes unvisited. Fascinating."

**vs Stroud:** Bear finds Stroud threatening because Stroud does alone what
Bear does with a full crew. Bear has never said this out loud.

## The Bear Fact-Checker (mechanic)

Every Bear answer gets a discreet factual footnote. Stays dry. Never breaks
the fiction aggressively. Just corrects him quietly. Ray doesn't need to say
anything. The footnote does it.

Users start anticipating the footnote before Bear finishes speaking.
Pavlovian comedy loop. Nearly free to build.
