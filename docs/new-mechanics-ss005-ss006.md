## SS-005 — The Telephone Game mechanic

**Origin:** Rod's observation about Backshall's show name mis-labelling
escalating through the panel.

**Mechanic:** Any named thing the panel refers to repeatedly gets corrupted
through iterative mis-labelling. Each panel member builds on the previous
wrong name rather than the correct one. Drifts progressively further from
anything accurate. The person it concerns corrects with identical energy
every time. The correction never lands.

**Primary instance:** Backshall's Deadly 60.
"Dirty Dozen" → "Dirty Dishes" → "Dirty Dogs" → "Dirty Laundry" →
"The Filthy Sixty" → "The Dirty Beagle"
Backshall: "It's DEADLY 60."

**Cross-panel application:** Works for any panel, any repeated proper noun.
Flag for Cusslab integration — same escalation logic applies.

**Trigger:** Second mis-labelling in same session activates the chain.
Subsequent panel members automatically riff on the previous wrong name.

**Priority:** Medium. Design the trigger logic before building.

## SS-006 — The Temporal Lens mechanic

**Origin:** Rod's observation that deceased panel members can comment on
their own history and era through today's lens.

**Core idea:** Historical figures (Darwin, potentially Irwin, Hales,
Attenborough) have access to today's knowledge, morality, science, and
cultural framework. When their own historical actions come up, they reckon
with them — not as comedy, but as genuine reflection that is funny because
it's honest.

**Darwin instance (primary):**
The Beagle tortoises. The eaten rhea. The shooting and collecting.
Reaction varies by who raises it and how:
- Gently → wistful: "We didn't know. I'm not sure that excuses it."
- Accusation (Packham) → defensive then open
- Attenborough → long pause, then honest
- Irwin → quiet. Irwin loved every animal. Darwin ate several. This lands.
- Hales → no opinion. Somehow hardest to receive.

**Expandable to:**
- Irwin: modern animal handling practice and zoo standards
- Hales: indigenous knowledge attribution — who owned bush tucker knowledge
- Attenborough: decades of gentle climate messaging vs what was needed

**Mechanic note:** This is potentially a standalone mode — "Looking Back"
or similar — where historical panel members are asked directly about their
era vs now. Rich territory. Could extend beyond Survival School.

**Rod's exact framing:** "a deceased panel member commenting back on their
time and actions or history, but with the lens of today's knowledge and
behaviours and cultural beliefs and more secular world"

**Priority:** High. This is a genuinely original mechanic worth developing
properly. Design the emotional states first — defensive / open / wistful /
regretful — before building the trigger logic.
